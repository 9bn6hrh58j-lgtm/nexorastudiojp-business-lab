# Recurring Utility Ranking V1

## Purpose

Adopt the conclusion from `REAL_PAYMENT_BEHAVIOR_ANALYSIS_V1.md`.

New highest-level evaluation axis:

```text
Recurring Utility
```

Definition:

```text
Does the user have a reason to use this every week or every month?
```

Revenue Risk Scanner receives no special treatment.

## Evaluation Axes

Each axis is scored 1 to 5.

| Axis | 5 Means | 1 Means |
|---|---|---|
| Desire intensity | User strongly wants the outcome | Weak or curiosity-only |
| Result certainty | Output reliably creates value | Value is uncertain |
| Recurring Utility | Weekly/monthly natural use | One-time or rare use |
| Hassle reduction | Removes repeated annoying work | Mostly information |
| AI-led operation | AI can operate most of it | Human work is central |
| Build vs Buy | Product beats ChatGPT/Claude/self-build | Generic AI is enough |

Maximum score:

```text
30
```

## Executive Judgment

Recurring Utility changes the ranking.

The strongest ideas are no longer the ones that produce a good one-time output.

The strongest ideas are the ones that become:

```text
a repeated operating habit
```

or:

```text
a dependable utility layer
```

Top candidates now favor:

- recurring records
- recurring reviews
- recurring monitoring
- recurring internal questions
- recurring compliance checks
- recurring customer communication
- recurring field reports

One-time generators and one-time diagnostics fall.

## Why The Ranking Changed

## 1. Recurring Utility Is Now A Core Gate

The owner's real payment behavior shows payment is easiest when:

```text
the service removes a recurring bottleneck.
```

Therefore, weekly/monthly workflows rise.

One-time generators fall even if the output is useful.

## 2. Text Generation Alone Is Still Weak

Several ideas score well on desire and certainty but remain weak because they are easy to replace with ChatGPT or Claude.

Examples:

- product descriptions
- ES generation
- social posts
- recipes
- travel plans
- ad copy

They may get free users.

They are weak paid businesses unless they own:

- distribution
- workflow
- saved context
- platform integration
- team use
- monitoring

## 3. Operational Records Are Strong

The strongest recurring utility pattern is:

```text
work happens repeatedly
-> record/report/reply must be created
-> AI reduces the work
-> history matters
```

This is why these rose:

- review replies
- internal FAQ
- construction reports
- care/nursing records
- treatment notes
- meeting-to-task workflows

## 4. Monitoring Is Stronger Than One-Time Diagnosis

Monitoring products have natural recurring utility because the user has a reason to come back:

- prices change
- job postings change
- patents change
- competitor posts change
- code changes
- reviews arrive

This pattern is stronger than:

```text
scan once and read a report.
```

## Revenue Risk Scanner Reassessment

Revenue Risk Scanner remains a candidate only.

Score:

```text
19 / 30
```

Why it is weak under Recurring Utility:

- a store owner may scan once and leave
- weekly/monthly usage is not automatic
- the result is diagnostic, not operational
- value is less certain than a reply, record, or monitoring alert
- ChatGPT/Claude can imitate a basic audit

How it could improve:

- shift from one-time scan to monitoring
- track changes weekly
- compare competitor pages
- alert before campaigns
- create recurring fix workflow
- save history and benchmark findings

Without those changes, it should not rank above stronger recurring utilities.
