# Human Desire Map V1

## Purpose

Analyze services that people use without long explanation.

This is not a Revenue Risk Scanner improvement document.

This document asks:

```text
What makes a person jump in before they understand the full product?
```

Target emotional conditions:

- I want to do this now
- I want less effort
- I want less anxiety
- I want more money
- I want more time

Reference examples:

- bulk quote / comparison services
- Uber
- Airbnb
- Mercari

## Executive Judgment

Services that people immediately use do not start from feature logic.

They start from an obvious human state:

```text
I have a desire, pain, or uncertainty right now, and this service looks like the shortest path to relief.
```

The winning pattern is:

```text
strong desire
low first action
fast visible reward
socially explainable result
repeatable trigger
```

The service does not need to be fully trusted before the first action.

It only needs to feel easier than doing nothing.

## Core Human Desire Categories

| Desire | What The User Feels | Service Promise |
|---|---|---|
| Get money | I may be leaving money on the table | Find or unlock value |
| Save money | I may be overpaying | Compare and reduce cost |
| Save time | This task is annoying | Do it faster |
| Reduce anxiety | I do not know what is correct | Give certainty |
| Avoid loss | I may make a costly mistake | Warn me before damage |
| Gain convenience | I want this without friction | Bring it to me |
| Gain status | I want to look smart or capable | Make me look competent |
| Turn idle asset into income | I own something underused | Monetize it |
| Remove negotiation | I hate calling or bargaining | Handle the messy part |
| Create optionality | I want choices without commitment | Show options |

The strongest services often combine at least three:

```text
save time + reduce anxiety + get/save money
```

## 1. Why These Services Are Used

### Bulk Quote / Comparison Services

People use them because they believe:

```text
I may be overpaying or missing a better option.
```

Core psychology:

- fear of overpaying
- fear of choosing wrong
- desire to avoid calling many vendors
- belief that comparison creates leverage
- low effort compared with manual research

Why it works:

The user does not need to love the platform.

They only need to think:

```text
I should check before deciding.
```

### Uber

People use it because the desire is immediate:

```text
I need to move now.
```

Core psychology:

- immediate need
- uncertainty reduction
- visible ETA
- price estimate
- no phone call
- no cash negotiation
- lower social friction

Why it works:

It turns an uncertain offline task into a predictable tap.

### Airbnb

People use it because it turns a complex decision into searchable optionality:

```text
I want a place that fits my trip, budget, and identity.
```

Core psychology:

- desire for better value
- desire for uniqueness
- more options than hotels
- social proof through reviews
- fear reduction through photos, rules, and ratings

Why it works:

It sells both practical utility and imagined experience.

### Mercari

People use it because it turns unused items into possible money:

```text
This thing might be worth something.
```

Core psychology:

- found money
- decluttering relief
- low listing friction
- small dopamine loop
- social proof from sold listings
- no need to build a store

Why it works:

It converts latent assets into visible opportunity.

## 2. Why People Register

People register when the next step feels smaller than the benefit.

Registration succeeds when it is framed as:

```text
the doorway to the reward
```

not:

```text
an account creation task
```

### Registration Drivers

| Driver | User Thought |
|---|---|
| Save result | I do not want to lose this |
| Get quotes | I need the answer |
| Receive matches | Someone will bring options to me |
| Track progress | I want to know what happens next |
| Message counterpart | I need access to the other side |
| Unlock price/value | I want the number |
| Reduce risk | I want proof before deciding |

### What Makes Registration Easy

Registration is accepted when:

- the user already took a meaningful first action
- the reward is visible but incomplete
- the next step is specific
- the user understands what happens after registration
- the perceived upside is immediate

Bad registration asks:

```text
Create an account to use our tool.
```

Good registration asks:

```text
Enter your email to receive the 3 quotes / save this result / get the buyer response / track your listing.
```

## 3. Why People Recommend It

People recommend services when the recommendation makes them look useful.

The strongest referral trigger is:

```text
I found an easier way to solve a common problem.
```

### Recommendation Drivers

| Driver | Why It Spreads |
|---|---|
| Obvious utility | Easy to explain in one sentence |
| Money result | People share savings or earnings |
| Time saved | People share shortcuts |
| Anxiety relief | People share certainty |
| Social proof | Others can verify the result |
| Repeatable use case | Many friends have the same problem |
| Status boost | Recommender looks smart |

### Example Referral Phrases

Bulk quote:

```text
Before you choose, compare prices here.
```

Uber:

```text
Just call one from the app.
```

Airbnb:

```text
You can find better places than hotels.
```

Mercari:

```text
You can sell that.
```

The best services have a referral sentence under 10 words.

If a service needs a paragraph to explain, referral becomes harder.

## 4. Psychological Pattern Behind The Examples

### Pattern 1: Hidden Value Becomes Visible

Mercari:

```text
Your unused item may be money.
```

Bulk quote:

```text
Your current price may be too high.
```

Airbnb:

```text
There may be a better stay than the default hotel.
```

Psychological pull:

```text
I might gain something by checking.
```

### Pattern 2: Messy Offline Work Becomes One Action

Uber:

```text
No calling, explaining, waiting uncertainly, or handling cash.
```

Bulk quote:

```text
No contacting every vendor manually.
```

Mercari:

```text
No opening a shop.
```

Psychological pull:

```text
This removes a task I dislike.
```

### Pattern 3: Uncertainty Becomes Options

Airbnb:

```text
I can see places, prices, photos, rules, reviews.
```

Bulk quote:

```text
I can compare vendors.
```

Uber:

```text
I can see driver, price, route, ETA.
```

Psychological pull:

```text
I feel more in control.
```

### Pattern 4: The First Step Is Almost Too Easy

The user does not begin with commitment.

They begin with:

- enter location
- enter item
- enter destination
- enter dates
- enter request
- upload photo

The first action is exploratory.

The service then converts exploration into commitment.

### Pattern 5: The Reward Is Fast

If the first reward is delayed, desire cools.

Winning services show something quickly:

- estimated price
- nearby driver
- available rooms
- similar sold listings
- quote expectation
- matched provider

Speed creates belief.

## Desire Map For New Business Ideas

Use this as a screening table.

| Question | Strong Answer | Weak Answer |
|---|---|---|
| What does the user want now? | money, time, certainty, relief | insight, improvement, awareness |
| What is the first action? | one input, photo, URL, location | long form, explanation, setup |
| What is the first reward? | price, match, estimate, option, warning | report, score, generic advice |
| Why register? | save result, get match, unlock response | create account to continue |
| Why return? | new need, tracking, alerts, market change | maybe check again later |
| Why share? | saves money/time, solves common pain | interesting tool |
| Why pay? | transaction, lead, saved loss, unlocked money | more analysis |

## Strong Service Archetypes

### 1. "Find Money I Did Not Know I Had"

Examples:

- sell unused goods
- refund finder
- subsidy finder
- overpayment checker
- tax deduction finder
- unused subscription cancellation

Psychology:

```text
I can get money with low effort.
```

High pull.

### 2. "Show Me The Best Option"

Examples:

- one-click quotes
- price comparison
- vendor matching
- insurance comparison
- loan comparison
- SaaS/tool recommendation

Psychology:

```text
I do not want to choose wrong.
```

High pull if money or risk is involved.

### 3. "Do The Annoying Task For Me"

Examples:

- booking
- paperwork
- scheduling
- reporting
- listing generation
- customer reply drafting

Psychology:

```text
I hate doing this.
```

High pull if the task is frequent.

### 4. "Make Me Look Competent"

Examples:

- proposal generator
- report generator
- pitch deck assistant
- meeting summary
- professional email writer

Psychology:

```text
I need to produce something acceptable quickly.
```

Medium-high pull.

Risk:

Often ChatGPT-replaceable unless workflow-specific.

### 5. "Protect Me Before I Make A Mistake"

Examples:

- contract risk check
- compliance check
- scam detector
- pre-purchase inspection
- launch readiness check

Psychology:

```text
I might regret this decision.
```

High pull when stakes are clear.

Weak if the risk is abstract.

## Revenue Risk Scanner From Desire Psychology

This section does not protect Revenue Risk Scanner.

It evaluates whether it naturally fits human desire.

### Current Desire Fit

| Desire | Fit | Comment |
|---|---:|---|
| Get money | Medium | It promises lost revenue, but indirectly |
| Save money | Weak-medium | Stronger if tied to ad waste |
| Save time | Medium | Easier than manual audit |
| Reduce anxiety | Medium | Gives confidence, but risk may be abstract |
| Avoid loss | Medium | Needs proof of real loss |
| Gain convenience | Medium | URL-only is good |
| Gain status | Weak | Not a strong ego product |
| Create optionality | Medium | Competitor/benchmark comparison could help |

### Current Problem

Revenue Risk Scanner's current implicit pitch is:

```text
Your site may have revenue risks.
```

This is weaker than:

```text
You may be overpaying.
You may have money sitting unused.
You can get a ride now.
You can sell this today.
You can find a better place tonight.
```

Reason:

The pain is not visibly present before the product explains it.

That makes it a harder sell.

### Stronger Desire Reframes

Revenue Risk Scanner may become stronger if it attaches to clearer desires:

| Reframe | Desire |
|---|---|
| Check before wasting ad spend | Save money / avoid loss |
| Find why buyers hesitate | Get money / reduce uncertainty |
| Compare your store to winning competitors | Status / certainty / optionality |
| Turn lost revenue into a fix list | Get money / reduce effort |
| Prove what to fix before hiring an agency | Save money / avoid bad decision |

Still, these are more complex than:

```text
sell unused item
compare quotes
get ride
book stay
```

So Revenue Risk Scanner has a distribution disadvantage.

## What "People Jump At" Looks Like

A service people jump at usually has this sentence:

```text
Get X without Y.
```

Examples:

```text
Get a ride without calling a taxi.
Get quotes without contacting vendors.
Get cash without opening a shop.
Get a place without using hotels.
Get certainty without doing research.
```

For a new AI business, the best form is:

```text
Get money/time/certainty without doing the annoying work.
```

## Business Screening Rule

Before building, ask:

1. Can a user understand the desire in 5 seconds?
2. Does the first action feel easier than doing nothing?
3. Does the first result create relief or excitement?
4. Is registration needed to receive a wanted outcome?
5. Would the user recommend it in one sentence?
6. Does the service attach to money, time, anxiety, or status?
7. Is the pain already felt before the product explains it?

If the answer to number 7 is no, the product is likely harder to sell.

Receive matches.

Referral reason:

People share easy matching.

## Strategic Implication

The best AI-led businesses should not begin with:

```text
AI can analyze X.
```

They should begin with:

```text
The user already wants money, time, relief, or certainty.
AI makes the path shorter.
```

AI is the engine.

The product hook is human desire.

## Final Judgment

Revenue Risk Scanner is not automatically disqualified.

But compared with the strongest human-desire products, it has one weakness:

```text
The user may not feel the pain before the scan.
```

That means it must either:

1. attach to a stronger trigger such as ad spend, launch, agency hiring, or competitor comparison
2. evolve into a broader money/time/certainty product
3. be deprioritized if real users only treat it as interesting

The strongest future business direction should be screened by:

```text
Does the user want the outcome before we explain the product?
```

If yes, distribution becomes easier.

If no, the product requires education, persuasion, and sales effort.
