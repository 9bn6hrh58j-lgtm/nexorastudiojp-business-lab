# Lead Market Competition Analysis V1

## Purpose

Evaluate three lead markets as market candidates, not final business ideas:

1. termite / pest control
2. exterior painting / roof inspection
3. commercial cleaning

The purpose is not:

```text
Is the market good?
```

The purpose is:

```text
Can a new entrant realistically enter?
```

## Evaluation Items

- lead price
- acquisition cost
- competition
- existing players
- barriers to entry
- AI-led operation potential

## Executive Judgment

The most enterable market is:

```text
commercial cleaning
```

The highest ticket but hardest market is:

```text
exterior painting / roof inspection
```

The best balance between urgency and operational simplicity is:

```text
termite / pest inspection
```

However, none should be started by simply building a generic quote site.

The only realistic entry path is:

```text
hyperlocal or micro-segmented qualified leads with clear buyer pre-commitment.
```

## 1. Termite / Pest Control Leads

## Lead Price

Likely range:

```text
10,000-30,000 JPY per qualified lead
```

Benchmark context:

- US pest control CPL references often appear around $35-$90 depending on channel, market, and quality.
- Some performance lead sources cite lower ranges for raw/shared leads and higher prices for exclusive or booked leads.
- Japan pricing may differ, but urgent pest problems and inspection/recurring contracts support paid lead economics.

The lead is more valuable when:

- homeowner or property manager is identified
- issue type is clear
- photos are provided
- location is serviceable
- timing is urgent
- consent to be contacted is explicit

## Acquisition Cost

Expected:

```text
Medium
```

Low-cost acquisition possibilities:

- local SEO pages for specific pest types
- seasonal content
- apartment/house owner community posts
- photo-based issue intake form
- property management outreach
- old-house / wooden-house maintenance content

Paid acquisition can become expensive if competing on generic keywords.

Avoid:

```text
シロアリ 駆除
害虫駆除
```

as broad paid-search entry points.

## Competition

```text
Medium
```

The market has:

- national and regional pest control companies
- quote/comparison pages
- local SEO competition
- emergency-service pages
- review/ranking sites

But it is less saturated than exterior painting.

## Existing Players

Representative categories:

- local pest control companies
- national pest control chains
- home-service comparison sites
- review/ranking affiliate sites
- local directories
- property maintenance companies

Japan search results show multiple ranking and comparison-style pages for termite/pest services, but the category appears less portal-dominated than exterior painting.

## Barriers To Entry

Main barriers:

- trust
- local coverage
- lead quality
- false or vague inquiries
- urgent response expectations
- provider willingness to pay
- seasonal demand

Regulatory risk is lower than legal/medical, but claims must be careful.

Do not present AI as diagnosing the pest or damage.

Position as:

```text
issue intake and provider matching
```

not:

```text
AI pest diagnosis
```

## AI-Led Operation Potential

```text
High
```

AI can:

- classify inquiry type
- collect photos
- ask qualifying questions
- estimate urgency level
- check service area
- generate provider brief
- route leads
- track response time
- detect duplicate/spam leads

Human role:

- provider onboarding
- lead quality audit
- dispute handling

## Entry Strategy

Do not start nationally.

Start with:

```text
one region + one issue type + 3-5 buyer providers
```

Example:

```text
wooden-house termite inspection requests in one prefecture
```

## Entry Judgment

```text
CAUTION / possible
```

This market is enterable if buyer pre-sell is confirmed first.

## 2. Exterior Painting / Roof Inspection Leads

## Lead Price

Likely range:

```text
20,000-100,000 JPY per qualified exclusive lead
```

Benchmark context:

- US roofing leads are often cited in ranges around $75-$250 or higher depending on exclusivity and market.
- Some contractor-lead sources cite roofing around $150-$350 in competitive markets.
- The high job value supports high CPL, but competition is intense.

Japan context:

Exterior painting and roof painting have many comparison and quote portals.

Search results show established services such as:

- ソトヌリ
- 外壁塗装一括net
- 外壁塗装の窓口
- various comparison/ranking media

## Acquisition Cost

Expected:

```text
High
```

Reasons:

- high job value attracts SEO and paid competition
- broad keywords are expensive
- users compare many providers
- existing portals have domain authority
- contractors may distrust shared leads

Cheap acquisition is possible only through micro-angles:

- specific neighborhood pages
- grant/subsidy timing
- disaster/weather-related repair context
- apartment/landlord maintenance
- roof inspection after specific events
- photo-based self-check content

## Competition

```text
Very high
```

This is a mature lead-gen category.

Existing players include:

- quote portals
- SEO media
- affiliate comparison sites
- local contractors
- review pages
- home improvement platforms

Many services already promise:

- free estimate
- multiple company comparison
- vetted contractors
- cost guides
- local matching

## Existing Players

Visible Japan-side examples include:

- ソトヌリ
- 外壁塗装一括net
- 外壁塗装の窓口
- 外壁ぬりかえナビ
- various local comparison sites

These players already occupy the obvious user journey:

```text
I need exterior painting -> compare estimates
```

## Barriers To Entry

Main barriers:

- portal incumbents
- SEO difficulty
- paid acquisition cost
- contractor distrust of shared leads
- high consumer trust requirement
- need for area coverage
- quote quality disputes
- sales follow-up speed

This market punishes weak lead quality.

## AI-Led Operation Potential

```text
High operationally, but not enough to overcome distribution.
```

AI can:

- analyze photos for intake
- structure quote request
- detect urgency
- prepare contractor brief
- compare estimate fields
- track response speed

But AI does not solve:

- traffic acquisition
- contractor trust
- portal competition
- local coverage

## Entry Strategy

Do not enter broad exterior painting.

Only consider:

```text
micro-niche / hyperlocal / event-triggered inspection leads
```

Potential wedges:

- roof inspection after storm
- rental property repaint planning
- small apartment owner maintenance
- subsidy/grant deadline pages
- second-opinion estimate comparison

Important:

Second-opinion and estimate-comparison may drift toward advisory/diagnosis. Keep it as lead qualification, not consulting.

## Entry Judgment

```text
WAIT / only micro-niche
```

High lead value, but not a good first market unless a very specific cheap acquisition wedge is found.

## 3. Commercial Cleaning Leads

## Lead Price

Likely range:

```text
10,000-50,000 JPY per qualified quote lead
```

Higher if:

- recurring monthly cleaning need
- office/clinic/gym/commercial facility
- contract size is known
- frequency is weekly or daily
- decision maker is identified
- current provider pain exists

Benchmark context:

- Cleaning/maid/home-services CPL benchmarks are often lower than roofing/legal but still meaningful.
- Commercial cleaning can be valuable because one lead may become recurring monthly revenue.

## Acquisition Cost

Expected:

```text
Medium
```

Cheap acquisition angles:

- new office openings
- clinics
- gyms
- coworking spaces
- restaurants
- schools
- local businesses moving locations
- property managers
- businesses hiring facilities/admin staff
- public opening announcements
- Google Maps categories

This market may be more accessible through targeted B2B outreach than pure consumer SEO.

## Competition

```text
Medium
```

Commercial cleaning has competition, but it is less dominated by giant quote portals than exterior painting.

Lead-gen competition exists, but many cleaning providers still rely on:

- referrals
- local SEO
- direct sales
- Google Maps
- relationship selling

## Existing Players

Representative categories:

- local commercial cleaning companies
- building maintenance firms
- office cleaning agencies
- facility management companies
- local directories
- B2B service marketplaces
- quote/contact-form pages

Compared with exterior painting, the obvious "consumer comparison portal" path is less dominant.

## Barriers To Entry

Main barriers:

- B2B trust
- recurring contract qualification
- location/frequency matching
- provider capacity
- lead quality disputes
- lower tech adoption among some cleaning businesses

However, barriers are manageable if the lead definition is strict.

Example qualified lead:

```text
Business with commercial premises, cleaning frequency need, service area match, decision maker contact, and permission to be contacted.
```

## AI-Led Operation Potential

```text
High
```

AI can:

- identify target facilities
- detect opening/moving signals
- classify property type
- qualify square footage/frequency
- create lead briefs
- route to providers
- monitor follow-up status
- generate localized outreach

Human role:

- partner onboarding
- lead quality review
- dispute handling
- relationship management

## Entry Strategy

Start with:

```text
one city + one facility type
```

Best wedges:

- clinics needing regular cleaning
- gyms/fitness studios
- small offices moving/opening
- coworking spaces
- beauty salons
- schools/after-school facilities

Do not sell "cleaning leads" broadly.

Sell:

```text
qualified commercial cleaning quote requests for [facility type] in [area].
```

## Entry Judgment

```text
GO for pre-sell validation
```

This is the most enterable of the three because:

- buyer count is sufficient
- lead value is meaningful
- acquisition can be targeted
- recurring revenue for provider supports lead fees
- competition is not as portal-dominated as exterior painting

## Recommendation

## First Market To Test

```text
Commercial cleaning
```

Reason:

It has the best combination of:

- recurring value for the buyer
- enough lead value
- manageable competition
- AI-led qualification
- accessible buyer list
- lower regulatory burden

## Second Market To Test

```text
Termite / pest control
```

Reason:

Urgency and lead value are good, but seasonality and trust matter.

## Do Not Start With

```text
Broad exterior painting / roof leads
```

Reason:

The category is too portal-saturated.

Only test if using a micro-niche wedge.

## Next Validation Step

Create buyer pre-sell scripts for:

1. commercial cleaning providers
2. termite/pest control providers
3. micro-niche exterior/roof inspection providers

The question:

```text
Would you pay for this exact qualified lead definition?
```

not:

```text
Do you want more leads?
```

## CEO Judgment

```text
Commercial cleaning: GO for buyer pre-sell
Termite / pest control: CAUTION, test second
Exterior painting / roof: WAIT unless micro-niche
```

## Sources

- Home-service CPL benchmark sources show pest control and roofing lead costs vary widely by trade, location, channel, and exclusivity.
- Public contractor-lead benchmarks commonly cite pest control as lower CPL than roofing, while roofing/exterior work supports higher CPL because job values are larger.
- Japan search results show exterior painting has many established one-shot quote/comparison players, including ソトヌリ, 外壁塗装一括net, and 外壁塗装の窓口.
- Commercial cleaning discussions and benchmarks indicate recurring contract LTV can justify lead fees, but lead qualification and provider trust are critical.
