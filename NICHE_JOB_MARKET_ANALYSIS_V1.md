# Niche Job Market Analysis V1

## Purpose

Evaluate niche job markets that are hard to search on existing job sites:

- tattoo-friendly jobs
- night-work jobs
- male night-work / male high-income jobs
- other hidden-condition job markets

Important:

This is not a plan to build a job site immediately.

The first phase is market validation.

## Legal / Operating Caution

In Japan, business activities that receive job seeker and employer applications and mediate employment formation may fall under employment placement.

Paid employment placement generally requires permission from the Minister of Health, Labour and Welfare.

Therefore, the initial validation should avoid acting as a recruiter or placement agency.

Safer first-phase scope:

- market research
- anonymous demand collection
- employer interview
- job information aggregation
- advertising intent validation
- content / listing demand validation
- waitlist / opt-in collection

Do not charge success fees for placements before legal structure is reviewed.

## Executive Judgment

The strongest market among the three is:

```text
tattoo-friendly jobs
```

Reason:

- clear search pain
- stigma/filter mismatch exists
- existing general job sites do not handle it well
- demand spans multiple industries, not only night-work
- can start with validation content and employer interviews
- lower regulatory/social risk than night-work

Night-work and male night-work have stronger wage desire and existing market demand, but also stronger competition, reputation risk, and compliance sensitivity.

The recommended first validation is:

```text
Tattoo OK Job Demand Map
```

not:

```text
job matching site
```

## 1. Tattoo-Friendly Jobs

## Market Size

```text
Medium
```

This is not a massive standalone job category, but it is a cross-cutting condition across many job types:

- apparel
- beauty
- bar/cafe
- creative roles
- construction/trades
- logistics
- warehouse
- hospitality
- foreigner-friendly workplaces
- night-work
- event/street culture jobs

Existing evidence:

- Some apparel job platforms already expose "tattoo OK" filters or pages.
- Reddit and Japan-life discussions show continued uncertainty and anxiety around tattoos and work in Japan.
- General job boards often do not make tattoo acceptance easy to search.

## Job Seeker Pain

```text
High
```

Pain points:

- cannot tell whether visible tattoos are accepted
- fear of rejection after interview
- unclear rules: covered vs visible, small vs large, hand/neck tattoos
- stigma differs by industry and company
- job listings rarely state exact tattoo policy
- foreign workers may not understand local norms
- people waste time applying to unsuitable employers

This is strong because the search condition is sensitive.

People may not want to ask directly.

## Employer Pain

```text
Medium
```

Employers that are tattoo-friendly may want:

- applicants who self-select correctly
- fewer awkward interviews
- younger/creative applicants
- differentiation from conservative employers
- more applicants in labor-short roles

But many employers may avoid publishing tattoo policies because of brand concerns.

## Success Fee / Revenue Potential

Direct placement success fee:

```text
possible but legally sensitive
```

Safer early revenue:

- employer listing fee
- sponsored listing
- tattoo-policy verification badge
- anonymous job seeker demand report
- paid employer survey/report
- ad/affiliate to general job platforms where allowed

Likely early price:

```text
5,000-30,000 JPY per employer validation/listing package
```

Longer-term:

```text
monthly employer listings
```

or:

```text
recruiting partnerships after legal review
```

## Acquisition Difficulty

```text
Medium
```

Job seeker acquisition channels:

- TikTok / Instagram
- Reddit / communities
- tattoo studios
- piercing / street fashion communities
- foreigner communities
- apparel / beauty communities
- search pages around "tattoo OK job"

Employer acquisition:

- apparel stores
- salons
- bars/cafes
- studios
- creative agencies
- logistics/warehouse
- construction/trades

## Build vs Buy

```text
Medium-high
```

Existing job sites can list jobs, but they do not usually solve the sensitive search condition.

The product wins if it owns:

- verified tattoo policy
- visible/covered distinction
- industry map
- anonymous applicant Q&A
- employer badge
- policy templates

## AI-Led Operation

```text
High
```

AI can:

- scrape/collect public job listings
- detect tattoo-friendly language
- classify industries
- generate employer survey emails
- summarize policy differences
- build city/industry maps
- answer job seeker questions
- create content pages

Human needed:

- employer verification
- policy quality review
- legal/compliance review

## Entry Strategy

Do not build job site first.

Validate:

1. collect 100 job seeker responses
2. interview 20 tattoo-friendly employers
3. publish a city/industry "tattoo OK job map"
4. test employer paid listing interest

## Judgment

```text
GO for validation
```

Best initial niche.

## Market Validation Plan

## Do Not Build First

Do not build:

- job board
- matching platform
- recruiting agency
- placement service

before validation.

## Phase 1: Demand Proof

Create:

```text
Tattoo OK Job Demand Survey
```

Collect:

- city
- tattoo visibility
- desired job type
- acceptable industries
- current search pain
- whether they would use a verified list
- whether they would pay for access/support

Target:

```text
100 job seeker responses
```

## Phase 2: Employer Proof

Interview:

```text
20 employers
```

Ask:

- do you accept tattoos?
- visible or covered only?
- which roles?
- would you list this condition publicly?
- would you pay for applicants who self-select?
- would you pay for a verified badge/listing?

## Phase 3: Paid Validation

Do not charge placement success fee.

Test:

- employer listing fee
- verified appearance-flexible badge
- sponsored listing
- paid hiring demand report
- niche ad placement

## Revenue Models

Safer early models:

| Model | Notes |
|---|---|
| Employer listing fee | Lower legal risk than placement if no mediation |

Riskier models:

| Model | Risk |
