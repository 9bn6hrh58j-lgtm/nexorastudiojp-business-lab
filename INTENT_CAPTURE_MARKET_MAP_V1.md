# Intent Capture Market Map V1

## Purpose

Evaluate high-intent markets where revenue can come from:

- user payment
- success fee
- affiliate
- advertising

New core axis:

```text
Intent Strength
```

Definition:

```text
How clearly the user is already trying to complete a high-value action.
```

This document evaluates market entry possibility, not just market attractiveness.

## Revenue Models Allowed

| Model | Meaning |
|---|---|
| User payment | User pays for convenience, comparison, document, or support |
| Success fee | Partner pays only after lead, contract, sale, or conversion |
| Affiliate | Commission from tracked signup/application/purchase |
| Advertising | Monetize high-intent traffic with ads or sponsored placement |

## Executive Judgment

The highest-intent markets are also the most competitive.

The best initial target is not:

```text
broad credit cards, broad insurance, broad job change, broad car sale
```

Those markets are already dominated by major comparison sites, SEO media, paid ads, and affiliate networks.

The better strategy is:

```text
capture high-intent micro-situations that major comparison sites underserve.
```

Best entry candidates:

1. Commercial cleaning quote intent
2. Pest / termite urgent inspection intent
3. Used car sale micro-intent by situation
4. Internet line switching for moving / remote work
5. Exterior painting micro-niche, not broad comparison
6. Job change in one narrow role/community
7. Insurance review only in narrow life-event contexts

## 1. Car Sale

## Intent Strength

```text
5 / 5
```

User intent is explicit:

```text
I want to sell my car for a good price.
```

This is one of the strongest consumer intent markets.

## Payout / Revenue Potential

```text
High
```

Revenue models:

- affiliate commission from car-buying quote services
- pay-per-lead
- success fee from sale introduction
- advertising on intent pages

Used car sale leads can be valuable because one acquired vehicle has resale margin for buyers.

## Acquisition Difficulty

```text
High
```

Broad keywords are heavily contested by:

- large car buying brands
- comparison portals
- SEO media
- affiliate sites
- paid search advertisers

## AI-Led Operation

```text
4 / 5
```

AI can:

- create situation-specific pages
- generate valuation preparation checklists
- qualify vehicle details
- route to partner services
- compare user priorities
- produce sale preparation guides

AI cannot easily beat incumbents on generic intent.

## Monthly 150,000 JPY

Possible if:

```text
10-30 qualified leads/month
```

depending on commission.

But traffic acquisition is the hard part.

## Monthly 1,000,000 JPY

Possible if:

```text
high-intent SEO or paid channels work
```

But this is a competitive affiliate market.

## Entry Wedge

Do not enter broad:

```text
車 売却
車 買取 一括査定
```

Possible micro-intents:

- moving soon and need to sell car
- shaken before selling
- inherited car sale
- non-running car
- company vehicle disposal
- rural car sale by area
- lease-end sell/return decision

## Judgment

```text
CAUTION
```

High intent and payout, but acquisition is difficult.

## Best First Tests

## Do Not Start Broad

Do not start broad in:

- credit cards
- insurance
- car sale
- job change
- exterior painting

These are high-intent but brutally competitive.

Only enter through:

```text
micro-intent + cheap channel + clear monetization
```

Human should manage:
