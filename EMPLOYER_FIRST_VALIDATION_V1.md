# Employer First Validation V1

## Purpose

Update validation order for `Appearance-flexible jobs`.

Old order:

1. 100 job seeker responses
2. 20 employer interviews

New order:

1. 20 employer interviews
2. 5,000 JPY listing test
3. job seeker survey

Reason:

```text
The payer is the employer.
```

If employers will not pay, job seeker demand alone is not enough.

## Scope Boundary

This is not:

- a job board build plan
- employment placement
- candidate matching
- success-fee recruiting
- interview arrangement

This is:

- employer demand validation
- listing / advertising validation
- information service validation

## Core Hypothesis

Employers with hiring difficulty may pay for clearly labeled appearance-flexible visibility if it helps attract applicants who would otherwise self-exclude.

The product does not sell:

```text
tattoo culture
```

It sells:

```text
more suitable applicants by removing appearance-condition uncertainty.
```

## Validation Sequence

## Step 1: Employer Interviews

Target:

```text
20 employers
```

Goal:

Determine whether employers have real hiring pain and whether appearance-flexible labeling has enough value to justify payment.

## Step 2: 5,000 JPY Listing Test

Target:

```text
3 paid pilot listings
```

Offer:

```text
5,000 JPY for a 30-day validation listing with structured appearance-flexible labels.
```

This tests payment before building a platform.

## Step 3: Job Seeker Survey

Only after employer payment signal.

Target:

```text
100 job seeker responses
```

Goal:

Confirm that the labels increase application willingness.

## Employer Target List

Start with employers where appearance flexibility may be realistic and hiring pain may exist:

- apparel shops
- hair salons
- nail salons
- tattoo/piercing studios
- cafes/bars
- kitchens/restaurants
- logistics/warehouse
- event setup
- construction/trades
- cleaning companies
- creative studios
- foreigner-friendly businesses

Avoid first:

- large corporate employers
- heavily regulated workplaces
- schools/childcare
- healthcare front desk
- conservative luxury hospitality

## Confirmation Item 1: Is There Applicant Shortage?

## Question

```text
Are you currently struggling to get enough applicants for part-time, shift, or frontline roles?
```

## Follow-Up Questions

1. Which role is hardest to hire?
2. How many applicants do you usually get per month?
3. How many hires do you need?
4. What percentage of applicants are unsuitable?
5. Are no-shows or early quits a problem?
6. What channels do you currently use?
7. What do you pay now for求人掲載 or hiring?

## Strong Signal

Employer says:

```text
We need more applicants and current channels are not enough.
```

## Weak Signal

Employer says:

```text
Hiring is fine.
```

or:

```text
We only hire through referrals.
```

## Confirmation Item 2: Tattoo OK Display Value

## Question

```text
Would clearly showing tattoo/piercing/hair-color acceptance help attract applicants who might otherwise not apply?
```

## Follow-Up Questions

1. Do you currently accept tattoos?
2. Covered only or visible OK?
3. Are hand/neck/face tattoos different?
4. Are piercings OK?
5. Are bright hair colors OK?
6. Are nails/beards OK?
7. Are rules different by role?
8. Would you publicly state these rules?
9. Do applicants ask about appearance before applying?
10. Have you had appearance-related interview mismatches?

## Strong Signal

Employer says:

```text
Yes, applicants may self-exclude, and clear labels would help.
```

## Weak Signal

Employer says:

```text
We do not want to show that publicly.
```

or:

```text
Appearance flexibility is not relevant to hiring.
```

## Confirmation Item 3: Monthly 5,000-10,000 JPY Listing Value

## Question

```text
Would you pay 5,000-10,000 JPY/month to be listed as an appearance-flexible employer if the page reaches relevant job seekers?
```

## Follow-Up Questions

1. What would make 5,000 JPY/month acceptable?
2. What result would you expect?
3. How many clicks/applicants would justify it?
4. Would you pay before applicant volume is proven?
5. Would you prefer monthly listing, sponsored listing, or one-time verification?
6. Would 10,000 JPY be too high?
7. Would a free pilot then paid renewal be more acceptable?

## Strong Signal

Employer says:

```text
I would pay 5,000 JPY for a pilot listing.
```

Very strong:

```text
They pay immediately or agree to invoice/payment link.
```

## Weak Signal

Employer says:

```text
Maybe after you have users.
```

This is interest, not validation.

## 5,000 JPY Listing Test

## Offer

```text
Appearance-Flexible Pilot Listing
30 days
5,000 JPY
```

## Includes

- employer name
- location
- role type
- application link or contact link
- structured appearance labels
- short workplace note
- no candidate mediation
- no interview arrangement
- no success fee

## Structured Labels

Use:

- tattoo OK: covered only
- tattoo OK: visible small
- tattoo OK: visible broad
- piercing OK
- hair color OK
- nail OK
- beard OK
- no-customer-facing role
- role-dependent
- ask before applying

## Payment Signal Levels

| Level | Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Compliment only | No validation |
| 1 | Wants update later | Weak |
| 2 | Agrees to free listing | Weak-medium |
| 3 | Agrees to 5,000 JPY pilot | Strong |
| 4 | Pays immediately | Very strong |
| 5 | Asks for multiple listings / renewal | Excellent |

## Success Criteria

## Minimum Success

From 20 employer interviews:

```text
3 employers agree to paid 5,000 JPY pilot.
```

## Strong Success

```text
5 employers pay 5,000 JPY.
```

## Stop Condition

Stop or revise if:

```text
fewer than 2 of 20 employers are willing to pay.
```

This means employer-side pain is not strong enough or offer is unclear.

## Job Seeker Survey Timing

Run job seeker survey only after employer signal.

Reason:

```text
Job seekers are not the first payer.
```

Survey goal:

- validate label usefulness
- identify most wanted filters
- collect email/waitlist
- understand job categories

Do not overinvest before employer payment signal.

## Interview Script

## Opening

```text
Hi, I am researching whether clear "tattoo / piercing / hair color OK" job labels help employers attract applicants who might otherwise not apply.

This is not a recruiting service and I am not introducing candidates.

I am interviewing employers to see whether a simple 5,000 JPY pilot listing would be useful.
Could I ask a few questions?
```

## Core Questions

1. Are you currently hiring?
2. Which role is hardest to fill?
3. What hiring channels do you use now?
4. What do they cost?
5. Do you accept tattoos, piercings, hair color, nails, or beards?
6. Are rules different by role?
7. Would you publicly show these rules in a listing?
8. Do you think this would increase applications?
9. Would you pay 5,000 JPY for a 30-day pilot listing?
10. What result would make you renew?

## Closing

```text
If I prepare a small pilot page with appearance-flexible employer listings, would you be open to joining as one of the first employers for 5,000 JPY for 30 days?
```

## Data To Record

For each employer:

- company/store name
- industry
- location
- role hiring
- current hiring pain
- current hiring cost
- appearance rules
- public display willingness
- 5,000 JPY listing willingness
- objection
- next action

## Expected Objections

## Objection 1: "We do not want to advertise tattoo OK."

Response:

```text
Understood. Would a more nuanced label like "role-dependent" or "covered tattoo OK" feel safer?
```

## Objection 2: "We need applicants first."

Response:

```text
That makes sense. For a pilot, the purpose is to test whether this audience exists. What number of clicks or inquiries would make 5,000 JPY feel worthwhile?
```

## Objection 3: "We do not pay for hiring."

Response:

```text
Got it. Are you currently getting enough applicants through free channels?
```

## Objection 4: "Tattoo is not relevant."

Response:

```text
Would other appearance conditions like hair color, piercing, nails, or no-customer-facing roles matter more?
```

## Final Judgment

```text
Employer-first validation: adopted.
Job seeker survey: second-order, after payment signal.
5,000 JPY listing test: required before building.
職業紹介: prohibited.
```
