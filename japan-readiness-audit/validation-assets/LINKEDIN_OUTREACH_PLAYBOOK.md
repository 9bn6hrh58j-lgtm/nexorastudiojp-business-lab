# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook - Japan Readiness Audit

## Goal

Validate whether overseas small businesses will pay for a Japan Readiness Audit before building the full Japan Market Entry Toolkit.

The goal is not content creation.  
The goal is paid customer validation.

## Target Companies

Prioritize companies that already show some reason to care about Japan.

### Best-Fit Companies

- Shopify or DTC brands with international shipping
- Skincare, wellness, fashion, accessories, food, home goods, stationery, pet, or lifestyle brands
- SaaS or digital tools with global users
- Small businesses mentioning Asia expansion or international growth
- Brands already shipping to Japan but without Japan-specific FAQ or trust messaging
- Companies with English-only product pages but visible international checkout

### Avoid For Now

- Large enterprise companies with formal market entry teams
- Companies with no live website
- Businesses requiring heavy legal, import, medical, food safety, or regulatory advice
- Purely local service businesses with no international delivery or digital product
- Companies already deeply localized for Japan

## Buyer Personas

### Founder / Owner
Best for small brands where the founder still handles growth.

### Head of Growth / Marketing
Good for SaaS, ecommerce, and digital products.

### Ecommerce Manager
Good for Shopify and DTC brands.

### International Expansion Lead
Good if the company is slightly larger, but sales cycle may be slower.

## Targeting Signals

Look for these signals before sending a message:

- Website has international shipping
- Product could appeal to Japanese consumers
- Japan is listed in shipping destinations
- Brand posts about global expansion
- Founder is active on LinkedIn
- Site has weak FAQ, shipping, or return clarity
- Site has no Japan-specific buyer reassurance
- Product category has strong Japanese consumer interest

## Outreach Principle

Do not start with "Do you want to enter Japan?"

Start with a specific observation from their website.

Bad:

> We help companies enter Japan. Are you interested?

Better:

> I noticed your store ships internationally, but I could not find a clear Japan shipping/customs explanation. That may create hesitation for Japanese buyers.

## Outreach Sequence

### Step 1: Identify Company

Record:

- Company name
- Website URL
- Country
- Product category
- Japan relevance signal
- LinkedIn contact
- One specific trust/localization issue

### Step 2: Send Connection Request

Keep it short and specific.

Template:

```text
Hi [Name], I came across [Company] and noticed you sell internationally. I focus on Japan readiness for small brands and would be glad to connect.
```

Alternative:

```text
Hi [Name], I liked what [Company] is building. I work on Japan market readiness for small ecommerce brands and wanted to connect.
```

### Step 3: First Message After Acceptance

Send a useful observation, not a pitch.

Template:

```text
Thanks for connecting, [Name].

I took a quick look at [Company]. One thing that may matter for Japanese buyers: [specific issue].

For example, Japanese customers often look for [shipping/returns/payment/safety/support detail] before buying from an overseas brand.

I am currently running a Founder Beta for a Japan Readiness Audit, where I review a site and identify the trust, localization, FAQ, shipping, payment, and return issues that may block Japanese buyers.

First 10 audits are $49.

Would you like me to send the details?
```

### Step 4: If They Ask For Details

Template:

```text
The audit is a written review of your current website from a Japanese buyer's perspective.

It covers:
- trust signals
- localization gaps
- FAQ gaps
- payment, shipping, and return clarity
- priority-ranked improvements

It is not a full market research report or translation project. It is meant to show what should be fixed before spending money on Japan launch activity.

Founder Beta price is $49 for the first 10 companies. Delivery is 3-5 business days.

If useful, you can send your website URL and I can confirm whether it is a good fit.
```

### Step 5: Follow-Up 1

Send 3-4 days later.

```text
Hi [Name], quick follow-up.

If Japan is not a priority right now, no problem.

If it is on your roadmap, I can review [Company] from a Japanese buyer's perspective and show the highest-priority trust and localization issues before you invest in translation or ads.
```

### Step 6: Follow-Up 2

Send 7-10 days after the first follow-up.

```text
Hi [Name], closing the loop on this.

My main note from the quick review is that [specific issue] could create hesitation for Japanese buyers.

If you decide to explore Japan later, I would start by fixing that before full translation.
```

Do not keep pushing after this.

## Cold DM Without Connection

Use only if the profile allows messaging.

```text
Hi [Name], I reviewed [Company] briefly from a Japan readiness perspective.

One thing stood out: [specific issue].

This may matter because Japanese buyers often check [specific concern] before buying from an overseas brand.

I am running a first-10-company Founder Beta for a Japan Readiness Audit at $49.

Would you like the details?
```

## Mini-Audit Lead Magnet Message

Use this when you can give value before pitching.

```text
Hi [Name], I took a quick look at [Company] from a Japanese buyer's perspective.

Three things I would review before a Japan launch:

1. [Observation 1]
2. [Observation 2]
3. [Observation 3]

These are not translation issues. They are trust and purchase-confidence issues.

I am offering the first 10 full Japan Readiness Audits for $49 if you want a structured review.
```

## Qualification Questions

Ask only after they show interest.

- Are you currently selling to Japan or only considering it?
- Do you already ship to Japan?
- Do you have Japanese customers or inquiries today?
- Are you planning translation, ads, or a launch campaign?
- What is the website URL to review?

## Response Handling

### If They Say Yes

```text
Great. Please send the website URL you want reviewed.

I will confirm fit first. If it is a fit, I will send the Founder Beta payment link and collect any context needed for the audit.
```

### If They Say Japan Is Not A Priority

```text
Understood. If Japan becomes a priority later, the first thing I would check is whether your site clearly answers shipping, duties, returns, and support questions for Japanese buyers.
```

### If They Ask For Proof

```text
Fair question. This is a Founder Beta, so the price is low while I collect the first 10 audits and refine the format.

I can share a sample audit report so you can see the level of feedback before deciding.
```

### If They Ask Whether This Guarantees Sales

```text
No, it does not guarantee sales.

The audit identifies readiness gaps that may reduce trust or conversion before a Japan launch. It is a practical risk-reduction step before investing in translation, ads, or a larger market entry project.
```

## Daily Workflow

### Daily Target

- Research 20 companies
- Send 10 connection requests
- Send 5 personalized messages
- Log every contact in the target company tracker

### Weekly Target

- 100 companies researched
- 50 connection requests
- 25 personalized messages
- 5 sales conversations
- 1-3 paid audit attempts

## Success Metrics

Track:

- Connection acceptance rate
- Reply rate
- Positive interest rate
- Payment conversion rate
- Most common objections
- Most common website issues
- Best-performing company categories

## Validation Criteria

The Founder Beta is validated if:

- 10 paid audits are sold
- At least 3 customers give detailed feedback
- At least 2 customers ask about deeper help after the audit
- Common issues repeat across multiple companies

The idea is weak if:

- Fewer than 2 paid audits are sold after 100 targeted contacts
- Most replies say Japan is not a priority
- Buyers want free advice but do not pay $49
- Customers do not see the audit as urgent

## Key Rule

Do not build the full toolkit until paid audit demand is proven.

The audit is the learning engine.  
The toolkit should be built from real customer problems, not assumptions.
