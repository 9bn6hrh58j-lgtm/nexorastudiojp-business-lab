# Japan Readiness Audit - Sample Report

## Client

**Brand:** North & Ember  
**Category:** Shopify DTC skincare brand  
**Country:** Canada  
**Website:** https://example.com/north-ember  
**Audit scope:** Product page, homepage, checkout signals, FAQ, shipping/returns, trust signals

## Executive Summary

North & Ember has a clean brand identity and a product line that could appeal to Japanese customers who like minimalist skincare. However, the current site is not ready for Japan launch.

The biggest issue is not translation. The biggest issue is trust.

Japanese customers are likely to hesitate because the site does not clearly answer practical buying questions: safety, skin compatibility, total cost, delivery time, return handling, payment options, and customer support in Japan.

## Key Problems

### 1. The product page does not reduce purchase anxiety

The product page explains the product benefits, but Japanese customers will likely ask:

- Is this safe for sensitive skin?
- Are the ingredients common or acceptable in Japan?
- Is there any fragrance?
- Can I use this with products I already own?
- What happens if my skin reacts badly?
- Is there Japanese-language support?

These concerns are not answered close to the purchase button.

**Why Japanese customers may feel uneasy:**  
Japanese skincare buyers often compare details carefully before purchasing, especially when the product is imported. A beautiful product page is not enough if it does not remove practical concerns.

**Recommended improvement:**  
Add a "Before You Buy" section near the add-to-cart area.

Suggested copy structure:

- Suitable for: dry skin, normal skin, fragrance-sensitive users
- Not recommended for: users with known allergies to listed ingredients
- Patch test: recommended before first use
- Support: English support available; Japanese support planned
- Returns: clear policy for damaged or incorrect items

**Priority:** High

### 2. Shipping to Japan is too vague

The site says international shipping is available, but it does not clearly explain Japan-specific delivery timing, tracking, customs duties, or expected total cost.

**Why Japanese customers may feel uneasy:**  
If a buyer cannot estimate when the item will arrive and whether extra fees may appear, they may abandon checkout.

**Recommended improvement:**  
Create a Japan shipping block on product pages and FAQ.

Minimum information:

- Ships to Japan: Yes
- Estimated delivery: X-Y business days
- Tracking: Yes/No
- Customs duties/taxes: explain who pays
- Free shipping threshold: if any
- Carrier: if known

**Priority:** High

### 3. Returns policy is written for the brand, not the buyer

The return policy is legally useful, but not emotionally reassuring. It is not clear what happens if the item arrives damaged, delayed, or unsuitable.

**Why Japanese customers may feel uneasy:**  
For overseas purchases, return risk feels higher. Japanese buyers need to know what is covered and what is not covered before purchasing.

**Recommended improvement:**  
Add a short buyer-friendly return summary.

Example:

> If your order arrives damaged or incorrect, contact us within 7 days with photos and your order number. We will review the issue and offer a replacement or refund when applicable.

Also clarify:

- Whether unopened products can be returned
- Whether opened skincare products can be returned
- Whether return shipping is paid by the customer
- Whether support is available for Japan orders

**Priority:** High

### 4. FAQ does not reflect Japan launch concerns

The current FAQ answers general brand questions, but not Japan-specific buying concerns.

**Missing FAQ topics:**

- Do you ship to Japan?
- How long does Japan delivery take?
- Will I pay customs duties or import taxes?
- Can I pay with credit card/PayPal/Apple Pay?
- Is the product suitable for sensitive skin?
- Are instructions available in Japanese?
- What should I do if my package is delayed?
- What should I do if the product arrives damaged?

**Recommended improvement:**  
Add a "For Japan Customers" FAQ section.

**Priority:** High

### 5. Trust signals are too brand-focused

The site communicates brand taste, but not enough proof.

**Current trust gap:**

- No visible customer reviews near product CTA
- No press or third-party mentions
- No founder credibility beyond brand story
- No ingredient transparency summary
- No Japan-specific reassurance

**Recommended improvement:**  
Add trust signals near the product CTA:

- Review count and review highlights
- Ingredient transparency link
- Secure checkout statement
- Shipping/return summary
- Real customer photo or quote
- "Ships to Japan" confirmation

**Priority:** Medium

### 6. Japanese localization should not start with full translation

Full Japanese translation is not the first step. The first step is fixing the conversion blockers.

**Why:**  
If the English page does not answer Japan-specific buyer concerns, translating the page will simply translate the same weakness.

**Recommended improvement:**  
Start with selective localization:

- Japan FAQ
- Shipping/returns explanation
- Product safety notes
- Checkout reassurance
- Post-purchase support message

Full Japanese site translation can come later if traffic and purchase intent justify it.

**Priority:** Medium

## Improvement Priority

| Priority | Action | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Add Japan shipping and customs explanation | Reduces checkout abandonment. |
| 2 | Add Japan-specific FAQ | Answers the questions buyers will ask before purchase. |
| 3 | Add buyer-friendly return summary | Reduces perceived risk of overseas purchase. |
| 4 | Add product suitability and safety notes | Important for skincare trust. |
| 5 | Add trust signals near CTA | Makes the page feel safer and more established. |
| 6 | Prepare selective Japanese copy | Improves confidence without over-investing in full translation. |

## Recommended 7-Day Action Plan

### Day 1
Create a Japan shipping and customs block.

### Day 2
Rewrite the FAQ with a Japan customer section.

### Day 3
Add a buyer-friendly returns summary to product pages.

### Day 4
Add product suitability notes and patch test guidance.

### Day 5
Move review highlights and secure checkout signals closer to the CTA.

### Day 6
Prepare short Japanese support copy for shipping, returns, and product usage.

### Day 7
Review the updated page from a Japanese buyer perspective before outreach or paid traffic.

## Final Recommendation

North & Ember should not begin Japan promotion yet.

The brand can enter Japan with a light launch, but the site should first fix trust, shipping, returns, and FAQ gaps. These changes are lower cost than full localization and more likely to improve purchase confidence.

**Launch recommendation:** Caution  
**Next step:** Fix high-priority trust and checkout concerns before active Japan marketing.
