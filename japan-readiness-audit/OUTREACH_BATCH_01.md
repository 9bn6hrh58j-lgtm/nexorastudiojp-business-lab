# Japan Readiness Audit - Outreach Batch 01

## Objective

Get the first reply.

This batch is not designed to close a sale immediately. It is designed to start a useful conversation with a relevant overseas business that may care about Japan readiness.

KPI:

```text
1 reply
```

No new market research.  
No new product development.  
Only first-reply outreach.

## Sending Rules

- Send from the CEO's personal LinkedIn account, not a generic company account.
- Keep the first message short.
- Do not include the payment link in the first message.
- Do not attach the full offer in the first message.
- Ask permission to send the sample report.
- Use one specific observation per company.
- If the message feels like a pitch, make it shorter.

## Offer Framing

Use this framing:

```text
I noticed one Japan-readiness issue that may matter for Japanese buyers.
Would you like me to send a sample audit report?
```

Do not use this framing:

```text
We help brands enter Japan. Buy our audit.
```

## Expected Objections And Responses

| Objection | Meaning | Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Japan is not a priority" | Timing problem, not necessarily rejection | Acknowledge and leave a useful note. |
| "We already ship internationally" | They may think shipping equals readiness | Explain that readiness includes trust, FAQ, duties, returns, and buyer hesitation. |
| "We can use AI for this" | They see the audit as generic | Emphasize website-specific Japanese buyer perspective and priority ranking. |
| "Does this guarantee sales?" | They worry about overpromising | Say no clearly; it reduces readiness risk before launch. |
| "Why $49?" | They are checking seriousness | Explain it is Founder Beta pricing for first 10 slots. |
| "Who are you?" | Trust gap | Say this is prepared by Nexora Studio JP, a Japan-based business lab focused on Japan buyer-readiness for overseas small businesses. |

## Reply-Rate Improvement Ideas

1. Lead with a specific observation, not a service description.
2. Ask to send the sample report instead of asking them to buy.
3. Keep the first message under 90 words when possible.
4. Use the CEO's personal account and write like a person.
5. Avoid "Japan market entry" jargon in the first sentence.
6. Do not mention payment until they ask or request the sample.
7. If no reply, follow up with one concrete issue, not a repeated pitch.
