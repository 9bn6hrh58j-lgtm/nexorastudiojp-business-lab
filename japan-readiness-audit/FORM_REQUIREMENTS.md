# Japan Readiness Audit - Form Requirements

## Objective

Complete the Founder Beta application flow for the first paid Japan Readiness Audit.

This document defines what the application form must collect, what it should avoid collecting, and how the customer should move after submitting the form.

This is not market research.  
This is not new product development.  
The only purpose is to complete the application path.

## Form Purpose

The Founder Beta application form has four jobs:

1. Capture buyer intent from LinkedIn outreach.
2. Confirm whether the website is a fit for the $49 Founder Beta audit.
3. Collect enough context to prepare a useful audit after payment.
4. Route the prospect to payment or manual follow-up without adding friction.

The form should be short enough to complete in under 3 minutes.

## Primary User

The form is for an overseas small business operator, founder, ecommerce manager, or growth lead who is considering Japan.

Best-fit applicants:

- Shopify or DTC brands
- small SaaS or digital product businesses
- brands already shipping internationally
- businesses considering Japan launch
- businesses with a live website

Do not optimize the form for enterprise procurement or deep consulting discovery.

## Required Fields

Keep required fields limited to what is needed to qualify the applicant and prepare the audit.

| Field | Type | Required | Reason |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Name | Short answer | Yes | Needed for follow-up |
| Email | Email | Yes | Needed to send payment link and audit report |
| Company name | Short answer | Yes | Identifies the business |
| Website URL | Short answer | Yes | Core input for the audit |
| Country | Short answer | Yes | Provides market/context signal |
| Product or service category | Short answer | Yes | Helps evaluate Japan relevance |
| Are you currently selling to Japan? | Multiple choice | Yes | Qualifies current readiness |
| Do you currently ship to Japan? | Multiple choice | Yes | Critical for ecommerce/DTC |
| What are you planning for Japan? | Paragraph | Yes | Shows urgency and intent |
| What do you most want reviewed? | Checkbox | Yes | Guides audit focus |
| Consent / scope acknowledgment | Checkbox | Yes | Prevents expectation mismatch |

## Required Field Options

### Are you currently selling to Japan?

Options:

- Yes
- No
- Not sure
- We have had inquiries but no formal launch

### Do you currently ship to Japan?

Options:

- Yes
- No
- Not applicable
- Not sure

### What do you most want reviewed?

Use checkboxes.

Options:

- Website trust signals
- Product or service clarity
- Localization gaps
- FAQ gaps
- Payment clarity
- Shipping clarity
- Return/refund clarity
- Japanese buyer hesitation points
- Launch priority list

### Consent / Scope Acknowledgment

Required checkbox text:

```text
I understand this audit is a website readiness review. It is not legal, tax, customs, regulatory, or sales-guarantee advice.
```

## Optional Fields

Optional fields should be limited. Do not make the form feel like a consulting intake.

| Field | Type | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn profile | Short answer | Helps connect applicant to outreach thread |
| Current monthly website traffic | Multiple choice | Useful context, not required |
| Timeline for Japan launch | Multiple choice | Helps prioritize urgency |
| Anything else we should know? | Paragraph | Gives space without forcing long answers |

## Optional Field Options

### Current monthly website traffic

- Under 1,000 visits/month
- 1,000-10,000 visits/month
- 10,000+ visits/month
- Not sure

### Timeline for Japan launch

- Already selling
- Within 30 days
- 1-3 months
- 3-6 months
- Exploring only

## Fields To Avoid

Do not ask these in the first version:

- annual revenue
- exact ad budget
- full customer persona
- detailed competitor list
- full product catalog
- legal/import details
- long strategy questions
- file uploads
- password-protected access

Reason:

These increase friction and are not required to validate paid demand for the first audit.

## Response After Submission

### Confirmation Message

Use this message after form submission:

```text
Thanks for applying for the Japan Readiness Audit Founder Beta.

If your website is a fit, you will receive the $49 payment link and next steps.

After payment, your written audit report will be delivered within 3-5 business days.
```

### Internal Next Step

After submission:

1. Review the website URL.
2. Decide Fit / Maybe / Not Fit.
3. If Fit, send Gumroad payment link.
4. If Maybe, ask one follow-up question.
5. If Not Fit, politely decline or explain why the audit is not useful yet.

## Customer Information Policy

Collect only the minimum information needed to:

- identify the applicant
- review the website
- understand Japan intent
- send payment and delivery communication
- avoid scope misunderstanding

Do not collect sensitive information.

Do not ask for login credentials.

Do not ask for private business metrics unless the buyer volunteers them later.

- Anything else we should know?

### Section 4: Scope Acknowledgment

- Required consent checkbox

## Completion Time Target

The applicant should be able to complete the form in:

```text
2-3 minutes
```

If it takes longer than 5 minutes, the form is too heavy for Founder Beta validation.

## Success Criteria

The form is ready when:

- public form URL exists
- responses are saved to a linked sheet
- confirmation message is set
- required questions are limited to qualification and audit preparation
- scope disclaimer is included
- payment link handoff is clear

## CEO Approval Needed

Before outreach, the CEO only needs to confirm:

1. Which email should receive form notifications?
2. What is the live Gumroad product URL?
3. Should Fit applicants pay immediately, or wait for manual approval first?
