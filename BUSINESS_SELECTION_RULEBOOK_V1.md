# Business Selection Rulebook V1

## Purpose

Reset all business candidates.

All previous candidates, including Revenue Risk Scanner, are removed from priority memory.

Only the selection conditions remain.

From this point forward, every business idea must be evaluated by this rulebook.

## Core Principle

The company should not choose a business because:

- it was already discussed
- documents were already made
- code was already started
- the idea sounds interesting
- AI can build it
- the owner likes it
- ChatGPT or Codex already invested effort

The company should choose a business only if:

```text
it can create near-term cash, has strong human demand, can be AI-led, and has a credible path to 1,000,000 JPY+ monthly profit.
```

## Minimum Conditions

Every candidate must be judged against these 14 conditions:

1. Near-zero initial cost
2. First sale within 30 days
3. Repeatable 150,000 JPY/month
4. Future 1,000,000 JPY+ monthly profit
5. AI-led operation
6. Build vs Buy resistance
7. Explanation-free demand
8. Result certainty
9. Desire intensity
10. Customer access
11. Smartphone-friendly
12. Sunk cost elimination
13. Network effect bonus
14. Performance-based monetization bonus

## Scoring System

Each condition is scored from 1 to 5.

```text
5 = strong
4 = good
3 = mixed / unproven
2 = weak
1 = poor
```

Maximum base score:

```text
70 points
```

The candidate is then classified:

| Score | Judgment |
|---:|---|
| 60-70 | GO |
| 50-59 | CAUTION / validate quickly |
| 40-49 | WAIT / weak unless unique insight exists |
| Under 40 | STOP |

## Hard Gates

Some conditions are not optional.

If a candidate fails any hard gate, it cannot be treated as a priority even if the total score looks good.

### Hard Gate 1: First Revenue

If the candidate cannot plausibly generate first revenue within 30 days:

```text
STOP as current priority
```

It may be stored as a long-term idea only.

### Hard Gate 2: AI-Led Operation

If human labor is required for most delivery:

```text
STOP
```

Unless it is explicitly a short-term cashflow bridge with fixed scope.

### Hard Gate 3: Build vs Buy

If the customer can open ChatGPT or Claude and get a good-enough result:

```text
STOP
```

Unless the product adds:

- workflow
- saved context
- history
- integration
- monitoring
- benchmark
- compliance layer
- marketplace access
- result execution

### Hard Gate 4: Customer Access

If the company cannot identify and reach at least 20 likely buyers within 7 days:

```text
STOP / WAIT
```

No abstract market sizing can override this.

### Hard Gate 5: Sunk Cost

Past work adds zero points.

Already-built files, code, names, and documents do not increase priority.

## 1. Near-Zero Initial Cost

### Definition

Can the idea be validated without meaningful upfront spending?

Allowed:

- Google Forms
- Notion / simple landing page
- GitHub Pages
- Gumroad
- Stripe Payment Links
- free social posting
- manual AI-assisted delivery
- existing MacBook / iPad / iPhone

Not allowed as first step:

- paid ads
- hired developers
- paid data vendors
- hardware purchase
- inventory
- delivery fleet
- large paid APIs before revenue
- custom SaaS build before proof

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Can test with form + payment link + AI output |
| 4 | Needs simple landing page or light setup |
| 3 | Needs small tool or automation |
| 2 | Needs paid data/API or integrations |
| 1 | Needs capital, inventory, hardware, or staff |

## 2. First Sale Within 30 Days

### Definition

Can the company get a real paying customer within 30 days?

This means:

```text
actual payment, not likes, replies, or free users.
```

### Strong Signals

- existing reachable buyers
- urgent pain
- fixed-scope paid offer
- low-friction payment
- clear before/after deliverable
- buyer can approve without committee

### Weak Signals

- marketplace cold start
- enterprise sales
- app store dependency
- long onboarding
- vague free tool first
- requires trust not yet earned

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | First sale likely within 7-14 days |
| 4 | First sale plausible within 30 days |
| 3 | Possible but uncertain |
| 2 | Requires long setup or audience |
| 1 | Not realistic in 30 days |

## 3. Repeatable 150,000 JPY/Month

### Definition

Can the business repeatedly produce at least 150,000 JPY/month?

This is the minimum survival cashflow threshold.

### Acceptable Paths

```text
15 customers x 10,000 JPY
10 customers x 15,000 JPY
5 customers x 30,000 JPY
3 customers x 50,000 JPY
```

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Clear path with small number of customers |
| 4 | Clear path if outreach works |
| 3 | Possible but demand uncertain |
| 2 | Needs many low-price customers |
| 1 | No clear repeatable path |

## 4. Future 1,000,000 JPY+ Monthly Profit

### Definition

Can the idea scale beyond small cashflow into a real business?

This is profit, not revenue.

### Strong Paths

- subscription
- usage-based pricing
- B2B monthly plans
- repeat workflows
- API
- marketplace fees
- lead fees
- performance fees
- high-margin digital delivery

### Weak Paths

- one-off low-ticket templates
- manual custom work
- audience-dependent creator product
- low repeat frequency
- heavy support per customer

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Credible path with 50-300 customers |
| 4 | Credible path with 300-1,000 customers |
| 3 | Possible but hard |
| 2 | Requires scale/audience/ads |
| 1 | No credible path |

## 5. AI-Led Operation

### Definition

Can AI perform most of the delivery, monitoring, support, and improvement?

Human role should be:

- final approval
- edge-case review
- strategy
- occasional quality control

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | AI can deliver 80-95% |
| 4 | AI can deliver 60-80% |
| 3 | AI helps but human review is frequent |
| 2 | Human delivery is central |
| 1 | Basically a human service |

## 6. Build vs Buy Resistance

### Definition

Why would the customer buy this instead of building or using ChatGPT/Claude?

Strong resistance comes from:

- recurring workflow
- saved history
- structured data
- integrations
- monitoring
- alerts
- compliance
- benchmarks
- marketplace access
- execution after analysis
- team collaboration
- domain-specific templates

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Generic AI cannot replace it |
| 4 | Generic AI can help but product workflow wins |
| 3 | Some substitution risk |
| 2 | Mostly replaceable by ChatGPT/Claude |
| 1 | Pure prompt wrapper |

## 7. Explanation-Free Demand

### Definition

Does the customer understand the value before a long explanation?

Strong examples:

```text
Get paid faster.
Respond to bad reviews.
Find wasted costs.
Book an available slot.
Prepare a claim letter.
```

Weak examples:

```text
Improve your strategy.
Discover hidden insights.
Optimize your presence.
Score your readiness.
```

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Understood in 5 seconds |
| 4 | Understood in one sentence |
| 3 | Needs short explanation |
| 2 | Needs education |
| 1 | Abstract / unclear |

## 8. Result Certainty

### Definition

Does the user reliably receive a valuable result?

High-certainty outputs:

- message
- document
- checklist
- comparison
- report
- booking options
- saved workflow
- detected cost
- formatted record

Low-certainty outputs:

- vague advice
- prediction
- score without proof
- strategy suggestion
- subjective improvement

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Result is useful even if imperfect |
| 4 | Usually useful |
| 3 | Depends on context |
| 2 | Often subjective |
| 1 | Unreliable or hard to verify |

## 9. Desire Intensity

### Definition

How strongly does the user want the outcome?

Strong desire categories:

- get money
- save money
- reduce urgent anxiety
- avoid public damage
- save time on hated work
- meet deadline
- avoid penalty
- recover from mistake

Weak desire categories:

- curiosity
- self-improvement someday
- general optimization
- nice-to-have report
- abstract insight

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | User wants it now |
| 4 | Strong recurring desire |
| 3 | Useful but not urgent |
| 2 | Mild interest |
| 1 | Curiosity only |

## 10. Customer Access

### Definition

Can the company reach likely buyers without paid ads?

Access channels:

- founder network
- LinkedIn
- X
- Reddit / forums
- local businesses
- Google Maps lists
- public directories
- existing communities
- app marketplaces
- SEO if intent is obvious

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Can reach 50+ likely buyers in 7 days |
| 4 | Can reach 20+ likely buyers in 7 days |
| 3 | Can reach buyers but response uncertain |
| 2 | Requires paid ads or platform ranking |
| 1 | No clear access |

## 11. Smartphone-Friendly

### Definition

Can customers use it comfortably from a smartphone?

This matters because:

- many urgent tasks happen away from PC
- mobile-first users are less likely to build internal tools
- mobile usage increases immediacy
- smartphone workflows can reduce ChatGPT substitution if purpose-built

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Designed for smartphone-first use |
| 4 | Works well on smartphone |
| 3 | Usable but better on PC |
| 2 | PC preferred |
| 1 | PC required |

## 12. Sunk Cost Elimination

### Definition

Does this idea require ignoring previous work and judging only future expected value?

Every candidate must start at zero.

Prior work cannot improve the score.

### Scoring

This is scored inversely:

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | No sunk-cost attachment; easy to stop |
| 4 | Some prior work but decision remains objective |
| 3 | Prior work may bias judgment |
| 2 | Significant emotional/project attachment |
| 1 | Kept alive mainly because work was already done |

## 13. Network Effect Bonus

### Definition

Does the business become more valuable as more users, suppliers, data, or transactions accumulate?

Network effects can include:

- marketplace liquidity
- benchmark data
- template library
- verified provider network
- user-generated examples
- industry comparison data
- shared workflow standards
- aggregate pricing/savings data

This is a bonus category.

It should not rescue a weak business.

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Strong network/data flywheel |
| 4 | Clear compounding data advantage |
| 3 | Some data accumulation |
| 2 | Weak network effect |
| 1 | No network effect |

## 14. Performance-Based Monetization Bonus

### Definition

Can pricing be tied to measurable customer outcome?

Examples:

- percentage of savings
- percentage of recovered revenue
- lead fee
- booking commission
- success fee
- pay-per-result
- usage-based pricing tied to value

Performance pricing is valuable because it lowers buyer risk.

### Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Directly measurable outcome and success fee possible |
| 4 | Usage/value-based pricing possible |
| 3 | Some outcome-based element |
| 2 | Mostly subscription/fixed fee |
| 1 | No clear performance pricing |

## Weighted Interpretation

Not all categories are equal.

The most important five are:

1. First sale within 30 days
2. Repeatable 150,000 JPY/month
3. Build vs Buy resistance
4. Customer access
5. Desire intensity

If these are weak, the idea should usually stop.

## Decision Rules

### GO

Candidate can proceed if:

- total score is 60+
- no hard gate fails
- first paid test is clear
- buyer access exists
- generic ChatGPT/Claude substitution is not fatal

### CAUTION

Candidate can be tested only if:

- total score is 50-59
- one uncertainty can be tested within 7 days
- no major upfront build is required

### WAIT

Candidate should be parked if:

- total score is 40-49
- attractive long-term but not 30-day revenue ready
- requires marketplace, data, or integration first

### STOP

Candidate should stop if:

- total score is under 40
- first sale is not plausible within 30 days
- customer access is unclear
- generic AI fully replaces it
- it survives only because of sunk cost

## Anti-Bias Rules

### Rule 1: No Prior Work Bonus

Previous files, code, documents, and discussions add zero score.

### Rule 2: No "AI Can Build It" Bonus

Buildability is not demand.

### Rule 3: No "Interesting" Bonus

Interesting ideas often fail because nobody pays.

### Rule 4: No Market Size Without Access

Large TAM means nothing if the company cannot reach the first 20 buyers.

### Rule 5: No Free User Vanity

Free usage is not validation unless it leads to:

- payment intent
- repeat usage
- measurable outcome
- referral
- saved workflow

### Rule 6: No ChatGPT Wrapper

If a user can solve it by opening ChatGPT or Claude, the idea is weak unless the product adds workflow, data, integration, or execution.

## Final Company Rule

From now on:

```text
Every candidate starts from zero.
Every candidate must pass this rulebook.
Revenue Risk Scanner is not protected.
Any old idea can be killed.
Any new idea can win.
```

The company is not looking for an AI feature.

The company is looking for:

```text
a high-desire, AI-led, low-cost, fast-revenue business with durable reasons to buy instead of self-serve.
```
