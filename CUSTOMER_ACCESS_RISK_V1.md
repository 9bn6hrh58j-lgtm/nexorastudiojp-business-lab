# Customer Access Risk V1

## Purpose

Evaluate whether Revenue Risk Scanner can reach the first 10 real users.

This document does not evaluate market size.

The question is:

```text
Can we actually get in front of 10 Shopify/DTC/ecommerce operators without paid ads?
```

## Current Status

From `REAL_USER_FEEDBACK_V2.md`:

```text
Real users interviewed: 0
Decision: Not enough data
```

The immediate bottleneck is customer access.

## Target User

First 10 users should be:

- Shopify/DTC founders
- ecommerce operators
- small brand owners
- ecommerce marketers
- Shopify freelancers/agencies
- CRO consultants working with ecommerce stores

Avoid:

- generic AI enthusiasts
- people without ecommerce sites
- enterprise teams requiring procurement
- people who will only give polite non-market feedback

## 1. Customer Access Methods

### Method 1: Warm Founder / Operator Contacts

Description:

Ask known founders/operators or second-degree contacts to review top findings.

Message:

```text
I am testing a URL-only ecommerce pre-audit that looks for buyer-facing revenue risks. Could I run one store URL and ask whether the top 3 findings are useful, obvious, or wrong?
```

Strength:

- fastest path to first feedback
- trust barrier is lower
- higher chance of detailed response

Weakness:

- may be too friendly
- may not represent market
- can produce polite feedback

Access risk:

```text
Low
```

Expected contribution:

```text
2-3 users
```

### Method 2: Shopify / Ecommerce Agencies

Description:

Ask small Shopify agencies, CRO freelancers, and ecommerce consultants to judge finding quality.

Why they matter:

- they see many stores
- they can tell if findings are obvious
- they may become API/partner users later

Strength:

- high-quality feedback
- can evaluate build-vs-buy risk
- may validate agency workflow

Weakness:

- they may build internally
- they may see this as competition
- some may not share client examples

Access risk:

```text
Medium
```

Expected contribution:

```text
2 users
```

### Method 3: Public DTC Store Founder Outreach

Description:

Find public ecommerce brands and contact founders/operators via LinkedIn, contact forms, or public email.

Positioning:

Do not sell.

Ask for feedback on a finding.

Strength:

- real target users
- tests cold access
- useful for market reality

Weakness:

- low response rate
- founders are busy
- cold outreach may look like spam
- requires careful message quality

Access risk:

```text
High
```

Expected contribution:

```text
1-2 users from 30-60 thoughtful attempts
```

### Method 4: Ecommerce Communities

Description:

Use ecommerce/founder communities to ask for feedback on example findings.

Examples:

- Shopify founder communities
- DTC founder groups
- ecommerce Slack/Discord groups
- Indie Hackers-style communities
- Reddit only with caution and subreddit rules

Strength:

- can reach many relevant people
- good for objection discovery
- can reveal whether wording resonates

Weakness:

- self-promotion risk
- many communities dislike tool promotion
- responses may be shallow
- link dropping can hurt reputation

Access risk:

```text
Medium-high
```

Expected contribution:

```text
1-2 users if posts are feedback-oriented, not promotional
```

### Method 5: Manual Teardown Content

Description:

Publish anonymized teardown examples:

```text
3 buyer-confidence risks found on a public ecommerce homepage
```

Then invite store owners to request a scan.

Strength:

- demonstrates value before asking
- creates trust
- can be reused for SEO/content

Weakness:

- slower than direct asks
- requires good examples
- may not produce immediate users

Access risk:

```text
Medium
```

Expected contribution:

```text
1 user initially, more later
```

### Method 6: Existing Business / AI Network

Description:

Ask people in the existing network who may know ecommerce operators.

Strength:

- low friction
- can create introductions
- useful for first raw feedback

Weakness:

- may not be target customer
- feedback quality may be weaker

Access risk:

```text
Low-medium
```

Expected contribution:

```text
1 user or intro
```

## 4. Shopify/DTC Access Difficulty

### Difficulty Rating

```text
Medium-high
```

Why:

- DTC founders receive many pitches
- "audit" offers are common
- AI scanner language is easy to dismiss
- founders may already use ChatGPT/Claude
- agencies may protect their methodology

### What Lowers Difficulty

- do not sell
- show one concrete finding first
- ask for critique, not purchase
- use buyer-confidence language
- avoid "AI audit"
- ask for 5-minute feedback
- focus on whether the finding is useful/obvious/wrong

### What Raises Difficulty

- asking for a call too early
- sending long messages
- leading with a tool link
- asking them to fill a form before seeing value
- talking about score
- using generic CRO language

## 5. Recommended Outreach Copy

### Warm Ask

```text
I am testing a URL-only ecommerce pre-audit.
It looks for buyer-facing revenue risks that analytics tools do not explain.

Could I run one store URL and send you the top 3 findings?
I only need to know whether they are useful, obvious, or wrong.
```

### Agency / CRO Ask

```text
I am testing a finding engine for ecommerce revenue risk.
It is not an analytics replacement. It looks for buyer-facing purchase friction on public pages.

Would you be open to reviewing 3 sample findings and telling me whether they would be useful in a client pre-audit?
```

### Cold DTC Founder Ask

```text
I noticed your store and I am testing a short ecommerce pre-audit focused on purchase friction.

Not selling anything here. If I send 2-3 buyer-confidence findings from public pages, would you be open to telling me whether they are useful, obvious, or wrong?
```

### Community Post

```text
I am testing a way to identify buyer-facing revenue risks on ecommerce sites.

Example: a store says "free returns" on the homepage, but the return policy does not clarify who pays return shipping or whether international returns apply.

For store owners/operators: would that kind of finding be useful, or too obvious?
```

## 6. Access Risk Assessment

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| No response from cold founders | High | use warm + agency feedback first |
| Outreach perceived as spam | High | ask for critique, not sale |
| Feedback from non-target users | Medium | require ecommerce role/store URL |
| Polite but useless feedback | Medium | ask direct useful/obvious/wrong questions |
| Agencies see product as threat | Medium | frame as pre-audit/lead triage tool |
| Users say Claude can do it | High | capture build-vs-buy reason, not argue |
| No one gives raw answer | High | reduce ask to 5-minute review of 3 findings |

## 7. Access Gate

Before building more features, pass this gate:

```text
10 real users reached
7 give feedback
3 report a useful/new finding
2 provide buy/build reasoning
```

If this gate fails:

```text
do not scale product development
```
