# Success Fee Business Map V1

## Purpose

Evaluate success-fee and performance-based business models from zero.

New rules:

- near-zero initial cost
- success fee allowed
- human work allowed
- AI acts as command center
- profit must exceed spend
- target 150,000 JPY/month
- future 1,000,000 JPY+/month
- SaaS assumption banned

## Scope

Analyze:

1. Recruiting
2. Lead generation / customer referral
3. Buy-sell brokerage
4. Contract brokerage
5. Other success-fee models

## Executive Judgment

The best success-fee model for the current company is not broad recruiting or regulated brokerage.

The strongest starting area is:

```text
micro lead generation / appointment-intent referral in a narrow high-value niche
```

Reason:

- near-zero start
- AI can research, qualify, route, and track
- human work can handle trust and closing edge cases
- payment is tied to measurable outcome
- monthly 150,000 JPY is realistic with a small number of qualified leads
- future 1,000,000 JPY is possible if the niche has repeat demand and high lead value

Best starting candidates:

1. High-intent B2B lead referral
2. Local service appointment lead generation
3. Small business buyer/seller matching for digital assets
4. Recruiting referral in one narrow role category
5. Sponsorship / partnership introduction brokerage

## 1. Recruiting

## Market Reality

Recruiting agencies commonly earn placement fees when a candidate is hired.

Public recruitment pricing references often describe success fees as a percentage of first-year salary, commonly around 15-35% depending on role, market, and region. Japan-focused recruitment fee references frequently cite roughly 20-35% for many agency placements.

## Why Customers Pay

Employers pay because:

- hiring is painful
- open roles cost money
- qualified candidates are scarce
- screening takes time
- success fee reduces upfront risk

## Desire Intensity

```text
5 / 5
```

Companies with urgent open roles feel real pain.

## Result Certainty

```text
3 / 5
```

Low until a hire closes.

Candidate quality, timing, interview performance, and employer urgency all affect outcome.

## Customer Acquisition

```text
3 / 5
```

Possible through:

- LinkedIn
- public job posts
- niche communities
- founder networks
- direct outreach to hiring managers

But competition is high.

## AI-Led Operation

```text
4 / 5
```

AI can help with:

- job post scraping
- candidate search
- matching
- outreach drafts
- screening questions
- CRM updates
- interview summaries

Human work remains important:

- trust
- candidate communication
- negotiation
- relationship handling

## Scale

```text
5 / 5
```

One placement can be large.

Example:

```text
6,000,000 JPY salary x 25% = 1,500,000 JPY fee
```

But cycle time is long.

## Profit Structure

Possible:

- placement fee
- referral fee
- retained + success hybrid
- candidate sourcing fee

## 150,000 JPY/Month Path

Possible with:

```text
1 small placement / referral every 1-2 months
```

or:

```text
3 candidate sourcing retainers x 50,000 JPY
```

## 1,000,000 JPY/Month Path

Possible with:

```text
1 mid-level placement/month
```

or:

```text
multiple niche placements per quarter
```

## Risks

- long sales cycle
- candidate trust
- employer trust
- legal and privacy handling
- strong competition
- success timing is uncertain

## Best Wedge

Do not start broad recruiting.

Start with:

```text
candidate referral for one narrow role where the owner can identify both employers and candidates.
```

Examples:

- bilingual AI operator
- part-time automation assistant
- Shopify operations assistant
- local business SNS operator

## Judgment

```text
CAUTION
```

High upside, but not best first 30-day revenue path unless a warm network exists.

## 2. Lead Generation / Customer Referral

## Market Reality

Lead generation and pay-per-lead models are established performance-marketing structures. Buyers pay for qualified prospects, booked calls, applications, or customer introductions.

## Why Customers Pay

Businesses pay because:

- customers are valuable
- outbound is hard
- ads are risky
- paying only for qualified leads feels safer
- measurable outcomes reduce buyer resistance

## Desire Intensity

```text
5 / 5
```

Businesses want customers.

This is explanation-free.

## Result Certainty

```text
4 / 5
```

If the definition of a qualified lead is clear, result certainty is high.

Examples:

- contact details + expressed need
- booked appointment
- completed inquiry form
- budget confirmed
- target geography confirmed

## Customer Acquisition

```text
4 / 5
```

Can start by targeting:

- local service providers
- agencies
- small B2B vendors
- consultants
- clinics/salons with appointment value
- repair/maintenance providers

## AI-Led Operation

```text
5 / 5
```

AI can run much of the system:

- list building
- prospect research
- landing page copy
- intake forms
- qualification questions
- lead scoring
- routing
- follow-up drafts
- reporting

Human can handle:

- partner onboarding
- quality checks
- payment collection

## Scale

```text
4 / 5
```

Scale depends on lead value and niche.

## Profit Structure

Possible:

- pay-per-lead
- pay-per-booked-call
- pay-per-closed-customer
- monthly minimum + performance fee

## 150,000 JPY/Month Path

Examples:

```text
30 leads x 5,000 JPY
15 booked calls x 10,000 JPY
5 closed referrals x 30,000 JPY
```

This is realistic if lead value is high enough.

## 1,000,000 JPY/Month Path

Examples:

```text
100 booked calls x 10,000 JPY
50 high-value leads x 20,000 JPY
10 closed referrals x 100,000 JPY
```

## Risks

- lead quality disputes
- attribution disputes
- low close rate
- spam risk
- compliance with outreach rules
- partner churn

## Best Wedge

Start with one niche where:

- customer value is high
- lead definition is clear
- provider can pay
- AI can qualify intent
- no license is needed

Examples:

- B2B service leads
- repair/maintenance quote requests
- specialty local services
- niche agency leads
- high-ticket education/training leads

## Judgment

```text
GO
```

Best fit for the new rules.

## 3. Buy-Sell Brokerage

## Market Reality

Brokerage exists wherever buyers and sellers need trust, discovery, valuation, or coordination.

Business brokers often charge success fees when a deal closes. Small business brokerage references commonly describe commissions ranging from single-digit to low double-digit percentages depending on deal size.

## Types

- business sale brokerage
- website / digital asset sale matching
- used equipment matching
- domain name matching
- inventory liquidation matching
- creator account / newsletter sale matching
- small local business buyer matching

## Desire Intensity

```text
4 / 5
```

Sellers want buyers.

Buyers want good opportunities.

## Result Certainty

```text
3 / 5
```

Deals are uncertain and can take time.

## Customer Acquisition

```text
3 / 5
```

Possible if targeting niche sellers:

- small websites
- micro-SaaS
- newsletters
- local equipment
- creator assets
- unused domains

Harder for regulated or high-value assets.

## AI-Led Operation

```text
4 / 5
```

AI can:

- identify sellers
- prepare listings
- summarize assets
- qualify buyers
- create deal memos
- manage outreach
- compare valuations

Human needed for:

- trust
- negotiation
- verification
- contracts

## Scale

```text
4 / 5
```

One deal can be meaningful.

But deal flow is hard.

## Profit Structure

Possible:

- listing success fee
- buyer introduction fee
- percentage of transaction
- fixed fee at closing

## 150,000 JPY/Month Path

Examples:

```text
1 small transaction success fee
3 deals x 50,000 JPY
10 smaller asset matches x 15,000 JPY
```

## 1,000,000 JPY/Month Path

Possible with:

```text
1 larger transaction/month
or
multiple niche asset deals
```

## Risks

- trust and verification
- legal/contract responsibility
- slow cycle
- low inventory
- buyer quality
- payment disputes

## Best Wedge

Avoid regulated brokerage at first.

Start with:

```text
digital asset / micro-business listing preparation and buyer matching
```

Still needs legal caution.

## Judgment

```text
CAUTION
```

Strong upside but harder first cash than lead referral.

- real need
- target geography
- budget or ability to pay
- timing
- consent to be contacted
- category fit

## AI Command Center Role

AI should manage:

- niche research
- prospect lists
- buyer/seller profiles
- qualification criteria
- intake form
- lead scoring
- routing
- follow-up drafts
- status tracking
- dispute evidence
- performance report

Humans should handle:

- final partner selection
- sensitive communication
- dispute resolution
- contract acceptance
- quality review

## Critical Risks

1. Low-quality leads cause churn.
2. Attribution disputes kill trust.
3. Spammy acquisition damages reputation.
4. Niche selection determines everything.
5. Some brokerage categories require licenses or legal care.
6. Success fees can create delayed cashflow.

## Anti-Mistake Rules

Do not start with:

- real estate brokerage without license clarity
- M&A brokerage without legal review
- regulated financial/legal recovery
- broad recruiting without niche access
- generic lead gen with no buyer lined up

Start only when:

```text
there is a buyer willing to pay for a clearly defined qualified lead.
```
