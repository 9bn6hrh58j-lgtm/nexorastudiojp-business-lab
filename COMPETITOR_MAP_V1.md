# Competitor Map V1

## Purpose

Identify differentiation points for Revenue Risk Scanner by mapping the competitive landscape across four adjacent categories.

Research date: 2026-06-03

## Category 1: Trust Audit Tools

### My Web Audit

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Price | Agency plan with $25/mo per additional user; base plan undisclosed (custom) |
| Customer | Digital agencies, SEO consultants, white-label resellers |
| AI use | No (rule-based audit output) |
| Strengths | White-label ready; branded domain delivery; GEO grid visibility; Google Search Console integration; Core Web Vitals tracking |
| Weaknesses | SEO-centric, not buyer-trust-centric; no cultural or localization angle; designed for agency resale, not direct-to-brand |

### Preuve.ai (White-label Audit)

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Price | Custom (enterprise white-label) |
| Customer | Agencies needing automated white-label audit generation |
| AI use | Yes (AI-generated audit content) |
| Strengths | White-label speed; automated output at scale |
| Weaknesses | Generic trust framing; no market-specific cultural trust signals; requires agency intermediary |

### Gap in this category

No tool specifically audits **buyer trust from a target market's cultural perspective**. All trust audit tools address technical signals (Core Web Vitals, SSL, speed) rather than psychological trust signals (payment clarity, return risk, support expectations for a specific national market).

## Key Differentiation Points for Revenue Risk Scanner

### 1. Pre-launch utility
Every behavioral analytics tool (Hotjar, Crazy Egg, Lucky Orange, Plerdy, Mouseflow) requires existing traffic to generate data. Revenue Risk Scanner delivers buyer-perspective insight before a single visitor arrives.

### 2. Cultural specificity
No competitor analyzes a website through the lens of a specific target market's buyer psychology. Japan-specific trust signals (shipping clarity, customs fees, return handling, payment options, FAQ structure) are not covered by any existing tool.

### 3. Works on any website without installation
Hotjar, CRO Guru, and Plerdy require code installation or Shopify-specific integration. Revenue Risk Scanner requires only a URL.

### 4. Actionable without data infrastructure
Amplitude, Semrush, and Unbounce require analytics tracking, page instrumentation, or test infrastructure. Revenue Risk Scanner produces output from a cold-start URL review.

### 5. Trust gap framing vs. conversion framing
CRO tools frame problems as "conversion blockers." Revenue Risk Scanner frames problems as "trust gaps that cause hesitation before purchase"—a more specific and culturally resonant framing for cross-border markets.

### 6. Price accessibility
The $49 Founder Beta price is dramatically lower than agency audits ($599–$899) and below most CRO SaaS monthly plans, with no subscription commitment.

## Risks and Honest Caveats

- No competitor directly addresses Japan market readiness, which could mean: (a) a real gap, or (b) insufficient demand to build a product around it.
- Manual delivery caps scale. If Revenue Risk Scanner stays human-delivered, it cannot compete on volume with SaaS tools.
- The strongest future competitor risk is Shopify or a major CRO tool adding a "market readiness" or "localization audit" module to their existing platform. Differentiation based on Japan-specificity only is a thin moat unless it deepens into data, tooling, or proprietary frameworks.
- SaaS competitors with AI (Plerdy, CRO Guru) are moving toward automated audit generation. Manual audits at $49 will face cost pressure if AI audit quality improves.
