# Painkiller Analysis V1

## Purpose

Reevaluate Revenue Risk Scanner as a potential `Nice-to-Have`.

Owner signal:

```text
"I would use it if it were free."
"It would have been nice to use."
```

This is not a strong buying signal.

It suggests the current product may be useful but not urgent.

## Zero-Based Judgment

Revenue Risk Scanner is currently closer to:

```text
Nice-to-Have
```

than:

```text
Painkiller
```

This does not mean the idea is dead.

It means the current framing and value promise are not painful enough.

## 1. Why It Becomes Nice-to-Have

### 1. It Is Diagnostic, Not Urgent

The product says:

```text
Here are possible buyer-facing risks.
```

But the customer may think:

```text
Useful, but I can look at this later.
```

### 2. The Pain Is Indirect

Purchase friction may cause lost revenue, but the customer may not feel the loss directly.

Painkiller products usually connect to an immediate pain:

- ad spend is wasting money today
- checkout is broken today
- customer complaints are happening today
- sales dropped today
- compliance risk is urgent today

Revenue Risk Scanner currently identifies possible risks, not confirmed losses.

### 3. ChatGPT Can Produce Similar Surface Value

If the output is:

- audit
- checklist
- score
- suggestions
- copy fixes

then customers can ask ChatGPT.

This makes the product feel like a convenience, not a necessity.

### 4. It Does Not Own A Trigger Moment Yet

Customers buy painkillers during trigger moments.

Current trigger is vague:

```text
I want to improve my store.
```

That is weak.

Stronger triggers would be:

- before paid ad campaign
- before product launch
- before international expansion
- after conversion rate drop
- after high cart abandonment
- before hiring a CRO agency
- after customer complaints about shipping/returns

### 5. The Output May Not Force Action

If the finding is:

```text
You may want clearer policy wording.
```

the customer may agree and still do nothing.

Painkiller findings must create:

```text
I need to fix this now.
```

## 2. Why It Is Not Painkiller Yet

### Painkiller Criteria

A product is closer to Painkiller when:

| Criteria | Revenue Risk Scanner Current Status |
|---|---|
| Pain is urgent | Weak |
| Buyer already feels the cost | Weak-medium |
| Problem has a deadline | Weak |
| Existing workaround is painful | Medium |
| Output creates immediate action | Unproven |
| Customer pays to avoid loss | Unproven |
| Tool is hard to replace with ChatGPT | Weak unless benchmarks/history exist |

### Current Failure Point

The biggest failure point is:

```text
The customer may believe the finding is useful, but not urgent enough to pay for.
```

## 3. Difference From "Want-It-Now" Products

### Want-It-Now Products Have Clear Triggers

Examples:

| Want-It-Now Product | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Broken checkout monitor | checkout failure |
| Ad spend leak detector | campaigns wasting money |
| Chargeback/fraud alert | financial loss |
| Inventory stockout alert | missed sales |
| Policy compliance warning | platform/legal risk |
| Review reply automation | public customer complaint |

### Revenue Risk Scanner Current Trigger

```text
Maybe your store has hidden buyer hesitation.
```

This is intellectually interesting, but weaker than:

```text
Your checkout is losing money right now.
```

### Key Difference

Painkillers usually attach to:

- money already being spent
- revenue already being lost
- operational work already painful
- customer complaints already visible
- deadline already approaching

Revenue Risk Scanner currently attaches to:

```text
possible hidden risk.
```

That is softer.

## 4. Can Revenue Risk Scanner Become Painkiller?

### Possible, But Only By Narrowing The Trigger

Do not sell a general scanner.

Sell around specific moments where the pain is already active.

### Painkiller Wedge 1: Pre-Ad-Spend Risk Check

Positioning:

```text
Before spending on ads, find buyer-confidence issues that may waste paid traffic.
```

Why stronger:

- customer is about to spend money
- wasted ad spend is concrete
- deadline exists

Potential buyer:

- DTC founder
- performance marketer
- agency

Painkiller rating:

```text
Medium-high
```

### Painkiller Wedge 2: Product Launch Readiness Check

Positioning:

```text
Before launch, catch purchase-friction issues on product, shipping, return, and FAQ pages.
```

Why stronger:

- launch has a deadline
- mistakes are costly
- founder wants confidence

Painkiller rating:

```text
Medium
```

### Painkiller Wedge 3: International Sales Readiness

Positioning:

```text
Find cross-border purchase blockers before international buyers reach checkout.
```

Why stronger:

- duties/taxes/returns/shipping ambiguity is specific
- buyer hesitation is credible
- benchmark data could be valuable

Painkiller rating:

```text
Medium
```

### Painkiller Wedge 4: Agency Pre-Audit Tool

Positioning:

```text
Generate client-ready purchase-friction findings before a CRO or Shopify audit call.
```

Why stronger:

- agency has repeated workflow
- saves time
- can support sales process

Painkiller rating:

```text
Medium-high if agencies care
```

### Painkiller Wedge 5: Change Monitoring

Positioning:

```text
Get alerted when site changes create new revenue risks.
```

Why stronger:

- recurring
- monitoring is harder with ChatGPT
- creates ongoing value

Painkiller rating:

```text
Medium, but requires more product maturity
```

## 5. What Would Make It Painkiller?

Revenue Risk Scanner becomes Painkiller only if it can tie findings to one of:

1. current wasted spend
2. launch risk
3. conversion drop
4. cross-border checkout uncertainty
5. customer complaint patterns
6. agency sales workflow
7. recurring monitoring

### Required Proof

The user must say something like:

```text
I need this before I run ads.
```

or:

```text
I would use this before every product launch.
```

or:

```text
This would save my team time on every client audit.
```

Without this, it remains Nice-to-Have.

## 6. If Painkillerization Fails

### Decision Rule

If after 10-20 real conversations:

- users say "freeなら使う"
- users say "nice to have"
- users do not name a trigger moment
- users would not pay
- users say ChatGPT is enough

then:

```text
Revenue Risk Scanner should be downgraded.
```

Not necessarily killed, but downgraded from main business to:

- lead magnet
- research tool
- benchmark data collector
- feature inside another product
- agency-support tool

### Hard Stop Condition

Stop treating it as Tier 1 if:

```text
0 out of first 10 users show paid urgency
```

or:

```text
Most users say ChatGPT is enough and no benchmark/monitoring/fix-copy condition changes that.
```
