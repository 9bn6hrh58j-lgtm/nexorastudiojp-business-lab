## 1. Free Diagnosis Flow

### Flow

```text
Landing page
-> URL input
-> free scan
-> top 3 findings
-> email capture for full report / saved report
-> feedback question
```

### Free Output

Show:

- top 3 findings
- pages checked
- short evidence
- one-line fix
- confidence level

Do not overemphasize:

- score
- risk level
- dashboard UI

### Email Capture Timing

Use a soft gate.

Recommended:

```text
Show 1-2 findings immediately.
Ask for email to unlock all 3 findings and save the report.
```

Avoid:

```text
Ask for email before showing any value.
```

Reason:

The scanner has no brand trust yet. It must show value before asking for contact info.

### Email Capture Copy

```text
Want the full finding list and fix notes?
Enter your email and we will send the saved report.
```

### Feedback Prompt

After scan:

```text
Which finding was most useful?
```

Options:

- I did not notice this before
- I knew this already
- This is wrong
- I need help fixing it

This feedback is more important than the score.

## 2. SEO Initial Strategy

### SEO Role

SEO is a medium-term acquisition channel.

It should start now, but it should not be expected to produce all 100 scans immediately.

### SEO Wedge

Target low-volume but high-intent search terms around specific buyer-facing risks.

Avoid broad terms:

- ecommerce analytics
- conversion optimization
- CRO
- Shopify audit

These are crowded.

Prioritize specific terms:

- shipping policy checker
- return policy checker
- Shopify return policy audit
- ecommerce trust audit
- international shipping policy checker
- duties and taxes ecommerce policy
- Shopify FAQ audit
- product page trust checklist
- cross-border ecommerce checklist
- store trust checker

### Initial SEO Pages

Create 8 pages.

| Page | Purpose | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| `/shipping-policy-checker` | Detect unclear shipping terms | Run free scan |
| `/return-policy-checker` | Detect return/refund ambiguity | Run free scan |
| `/duties-taxes-checker` | Detect import fee ambiguity | Run free scan |
| `/shopify-trust-audit` | Shopify-specific trust scan | Run free scan |
| `/faq-gap-checker` | Detect unanswered buyer questions | Run free scan |
| `/product-page-risk-checker` | Detect product confidence gaps | Run free scan |
| `/cross-border-store-audit` | Cross-border buyer readiness | Run free scan |
| `/ecommerce-revenue-risk-scanner` | Main category page | Run free scan |

### SEO Content Rule

Each page must include:

- what the problem is
- why buyers hesitate
- examples of weak wording
- examples of better wording
- free URL scan CTA

Do not write generic blog posts.

Every page should drive a scan.

## 3. Reddit / Community Use

### Verdict

```text
Use cautiously.
```

Reddit can help with learning and early scans, but direct promotion is risky.

Official and community guidance generally discourages accounts that exist mainly to promote one product. Subreddit-specific rules matter. Source examples:

- Reddit self-promotion discussion and guidance: https://www.reddit.com/wiki/selfpromotion
- Reddit help discussions commonly warn that accounts used mostly for self-promotion are treated as spam.

### What To Avoid

Do not post:

```text
I built a tool, scan your store here.
```

Do not repeatedly drop the same link.

Do not use new-account behavior that looks promotional.

Do not DM people unsolicited from Reddit.

### Acceptable Community Strategy

Use Reddit and founder/ecommerce communities for:

- asking for feedback on the finding framework
- sharing anonymized teardown examples
- answering merchant questions
- offering 3 manual/AI-assisted observations when allowed
- asking if the finding was useful

### Safer Post Format

```text
I am testing a URL-only pre-audit for ecommerce stores.
The goal is not analytics, but finding buyer-facing reasons someone may hesitate.

Example finding:
"Your shipping copy says free shipping, but does not explain supported countries or duties/taxes. Cross-border buyers may hesitate because they cannot estimate total landed cost."

For store owners here: would this type of finding be useful, or is it too obvious?
```

Link only if:

- subreddit rules allow it
- comments ask for it
- the post is clearly feedback-oriented

### Communities To Consider

Use only after checking rules:

- ecommerce founder communities
- Shopify merchant communities
- DTC founder spaces
- indie hacker communities
- small business ecommerce spaces
- CRO/UX communities for feedback, not sales

### Community KPI

Do not measure:

```text
upvotes
```

Measure:

- scans from community posts
- comments saying "useful"
- comments saying "wrong"
- merchants asking for report
- merchants sharing stores voluntarily

## 4. Shopify App Store Priority

### Verdict

```text
Important, but not first.
```

Shopify App Store should be a medium-term distribution channel, not the immediate path to the first 100 scans.

Reason:

- Shopify apps need review and must meet quality requirements.
- The app store rewards trust, reviews, listing quality, and merchant value.
- The current product still needs stronger finding quality before app submission.

Official Shopify app information:

- App Store requirements: https://shopify.dev/docs/apps/launch/shopify-app-store/app-store-requirements
- App review process: https://shopify.dev/apps/launch/app-store-review/review-process
- Shopify App Store overview: https://shopify.dev/docs/apps/launch/app-store-review

### Priority

| Phase | App Store Priority | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 0-100 scans | Low | Need proof of useful findings first |
| 100-300 scans | Medium | Use scan data to design app workflow |
| 300+ scans | High | Submit only after strong finding quality and onboarding |

### App Store Readiness Criteria

Do not prioritize Shopify App Store until:

- 100 scans completed
- 30+ email captures
- at least 20 scans have finding feedback
- at least 10 merchants say one finding was useful
- top repeated findings are known
- scanner can produce 3 strong findings on typical Shopify stores

## 7. Finding Quality Improvement Loop

Every scan should produce learning.

### Required Fields To Track

For each scan:

- URL
- platform if detectable
- category
- pages crawled
- top 3 findings
- finding quality score
- email captured yes/no
- user feedback
- "did not notice" yes/no
- false positive yes/no
- paid intent yes/no

### Weekly Review

Review 20 scans at a time.

For each finding category:

| Question | Decision |
|---|---|
| Repeated often? | Keep or prioritize |
| Easy to refute? | Rewrite or remove |
| Evidence weak? | Improve crawler/snippet |
| Fix obvious? | Add copy-ready fix |
| Merchant surprised? | Promote in output |

### Kill Rule

If a finding category repeatedly produces false positives, remove it from customer-facing output.

Do not protect weak findings just because they make the score look complete.

## 10. Main Risks

### Risk 1: The Findings Are Too Obvious

If merchants say:

```text
I already knew that.
```

the product has not found value.

Fix:

Focus on hidden policy gaps, contradictions, and cross-border details.

### Risk 2: The Findings Are Easy To Refute

If merchants say:

```text
That is on another page.
```

the crawler is too shallow.

Fix:

Improve crawl target selection and evidence wording.

### Risk 3: The Product Looks Like Generic SEO/CRO

If users describe it as:

```text
an SEO audit
```

positioning failed.

Fix:

Use revenue-risk and buyer-hesitation language.

### Risk 4: Community Posts Get Treated As Spam

If posts are promotional, they may be removed.

Fix:

Share examples, ask for critique, and avoid link-first posting.

### Risk 5: App Store Work Starts Too Early

If Shopify App Store becomes the focus before finding quality is proven, it wastes time.

Fix:

Delay app store submission until after 100 scans and repeated finding validation.
