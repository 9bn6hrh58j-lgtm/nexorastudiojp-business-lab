# Mobile First Opportunity Map V1

## Purpose

Evaluate the hypothesis:

```text
Mobile-first SaaS for industries that operate mostly without PCs.
```

Conditions:

- AI-led operation possible
- usable on smartphone only
- no PC literacy required
- low internal build risk
- JPY 1M+/month possible

This is a new opportunity map.

It does not replace Revenue Risk Scanner unless customer validation later proves a superior path.

## External Context

External signals used:

- Deloitte Tohmatsu MIC Research covers the Japanese `deskless SaaS` market as a field-DX category. Source: https://mic-r.co.jp/mr/03460/
- Yano Research reported Japan's smart agriculture market expected to reach about JPY 45.52B in FY2025, up 15.2% YoY. Source: https://www.yanoresearch.com/press/press.php/4094
- IMARC estimated Japan's smart agriculture market at USD 1.3856B in 2025 and projected USD 4.46B by 2034. Source: https://www.imarcgroup.com/japan-smart-agriculture-market
- A construction DX survey by Nohara Group / BuildApp Research Institute noted that over half of construction industry respondents had begun digitalization. Source: https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000317.000019866.html

Interpretation:

The market direction is real. Japan has labor shortages, field-work digitization pressure, and smartphone-first operators. The problem is not market existence. The problem is whether a small AI-led company can acquire customers and deliver value without becoming a field-sales/support-heavy business.

## Strategic Interpretation

Mobile-first field SaaS is attractive because:

- users may not build internally
- smartphone workflows are natural
- AI can turn voice/photos into documents
- recurring workflows exist

But it is dangerous because:

- customer acquisition may require human sales
- onboarding/support may be heavy
- low PC literacy also means low SaaS self-service literacy
- industry workflows differ deeply
- many buyers may prefer LINE/Excel/manual habits

Revenue Risk Scanner is weaker on internal-build risk, but stronger on:

- self-serve distribution
- SEO/app/API path
- existing MVP progress
- low-touch operation

## CEO Judgment

```text
CAUTION / WATCH
```

The mobile-first field SaaS hypothesis is promising, especially cleaning and repair/maintenance.

But it should not replace Revenue Risk Scanner yet.

Reason:

Revenue Risk Scanner already has:

- MVP direction
- unit economics design
- validation framework
- benchmark moat strategy

Mobile-first field SaaS is still only a hypothesis.

## Recommended Next Step

Do not build.

Run a paper validation:

```text
Interview 5 cleaning or maintenance operators.
Ask whether they currently create reports/quotes from smartphone photos.
Ask whether they would pay for automatic report generation.
```
